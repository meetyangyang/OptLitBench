An existence result for a nonconvex variational problem via regularity
Irene Fonseca1, Nicola Fusco2 and Paolo Marcellini3;
Abstract
Local Lipschitz continuity of minimizers of certain integrals of theCalculus of Variations is obtained when the integrands are convex withrespect to the gradient variable, but are not necessarily uniformlyconvex. In turn, these regularity results entail existence of minimizers ofvariational problems with non-homogeneous integrands nonconvex withrespect to the gradient variable. The x-dependence, explicitly appearingin the integrands, adds significant technical difficulties in the proof.
Keywords
Nonconvex variational problems, uniform convexity, regularity, implicit differential 
equations
