Long-term planning versus short-term planning in the asymptotical location problem
Alessio Brancolini1, Giuseppe Buttazzo2, Filippo Santambrogio3 and Eugene Stepanov4;
Abstract
Given the probability measure ν over the given region, we consider the optimal location of a setΣ composed by n points in Ω in order to minimize theaverage distance  (theclassical optimal facility location problem). The paper compares twostrategies to find optimal configurations: the long-term one whichconsists in placing all n points at once in an optimal position, and the short-term one which consists in placing the points one by one addingat each step at most one point and preserving the configurationbuilt at previous steps. We show that the respective optimizationproblems exhibit qualitatively different asymptotic behavior as , although the optimization costs in both cases have the same asymptotic orders of vanishing.
Keywords
Location problem, facility location, Fermat-Weber problem
