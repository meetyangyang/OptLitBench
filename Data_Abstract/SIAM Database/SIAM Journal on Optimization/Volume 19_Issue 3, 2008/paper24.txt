Universal Confidence Sets for Solutions of Optimization Problems
Silvia  Vogel;
Abstract
We consider random approximations to deterministic optimization problems. The objective function and the constraint set can be approximated simultaneously. Relying on concentration-of-measure results we derive universal confidence sets for the constraint set, the optimal value, and the solution set. Special attention is paid to solution sets which are not single-valued. With many statistical estimators being solutions to random optimization problems, the approach can also be employed to derive confidence sets for constrained estimation problems.
