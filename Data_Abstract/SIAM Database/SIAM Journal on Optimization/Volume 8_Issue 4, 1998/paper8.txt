Nonsmooth Shape Optimization Applied to Linear Acoustics
Abderrahmane  Habbal;
Abstract
The purpose of this paper is to study the optimal design problem of a nonsmooth shape, applied to linear acoustics in a bounded domain. Within the classical Helmholtz model, we consider the maximum of the acoustical pressure in a prescribed sensitive area as cost function to be minimized. A given part of the boundary of the domain is considered as the design variable. The existence of subgradients is demonstrated, and a technique for computing them is described. It turns out that the classical (differentiable) sensitivity subroutines could be used in order to compute subgradients. The numerical results presented tend to show that the acoustical pressure is not very sensitive to small changes of the shape for low frequencies, but it is sensitive for medium frequencies, and one obtains an optimal shape for the L2  cost that is slightly different from the one obtained for the L{\infty}$ cost.
