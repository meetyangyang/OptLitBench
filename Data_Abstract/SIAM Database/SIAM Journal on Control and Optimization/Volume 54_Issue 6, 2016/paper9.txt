Controllability Metrics on Networks with Linear Decision Process--type Interactions and Multiplicative Noise
Tidiane  Diallo and Dan  Goreac;
Abstract
This paper aims at the study of controllability properties and induced controllability metrics on complex networks governed by a class of (discrete-time) linear decision processes with multiplicative noise. The dynamics are given by a couple consisting of a Markov trend and a linear decision process for which both the “deterministic" and the noise components rely on trend-dependent matrices. We discuss approximate, approximate null-, and exact null-controllability. Several examples are given to illustrate the links between these concepts and to compare our results with their continuous-time counterpart (given in [D. Goreac and M. Martinez, Math. Control Signals Systems, 27 (2015), pp. 551--578]). In particular, these examples show that qualitative properties of models in gene networks strongly depend on the discrete- or continuous-time framework. We introduce a class of backward stochastic Riccati difference schemes (BSRDSs) and study their solvability for particular frameworks. These BSRDSs allow one to introduce Grammian-like controllability metrics. We propose  a minimal intervention-targeted reduction in the study of gene networks  as a potential application of these metrics.
