Invariance Concepts in Infinite Dimensions
Ruth F.  Curtain;
Abstract
The concepts of $(A,B)$ and $(C,A)$-invariance are examined for infinite-dimensional linear systems. Examples such as reachability, A-reachability, inobservability and A-inobservability subspaces are given. These concepts are then used to solve various disturbance decoupling problems.
Keywords
$(A,B)$-invariance, $(C,A)$-invariance, observability, reachability, disturbance decoupling, infinite-dimensional systems,
