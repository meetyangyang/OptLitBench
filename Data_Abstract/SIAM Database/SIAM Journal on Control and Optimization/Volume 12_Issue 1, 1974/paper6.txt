Some Results on Max-Min Pursuit
James  Flynn;
Abstract
A pursuer P and an evader E are confined to a subset $\mathcal{S}$ of the Euclidean plane. E whose speed is bounded by $w \geqq 1$ wants to maintain the greatest possible distance between himself and P whose speed is bounded by 1. We will show that if $\mathcal{S}$ is a half-plane or a circle, then E can prevent the distance $| {PE} |$ from falling below its initial value only if he has a strategy under which $\left| {PE} \right|$ stays constant whenever P moves at maximum speed along a polygonal path. We use this result to characterize $d^ *  $, the least upper bound on the values of $\left| {PE} \right|$ that E can maintain for the case of pursuit in the circle.
