Relationships Between Various Markovian Decision Problem Classes
Gary J.  Koehler;
Abstract
In this paper Markov-type decision problems that are generalizations of discrete-time, finite state, and action stationary Markov decision problems either in allowing negative entries and/or placing less restrictions on the spectral radius are considered. Many of the results found in the literature of Markov-type decision problems were obtained by authors investigating one of several solution procedures for solving the related problem of interest. This orientation has led to a number of seemingly unrelated results. In this paper several Markov-type decision problem classes are given and it is pointed out where gaps still appear in an overall theory.
