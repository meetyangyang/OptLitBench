Envelope Correspondence and Its Application to the Design of Guaranteed Error Controllers
B. Ross  Barmish;
Abstract
In this paper, various problems of minimax error regulation are solved via the method of “envelope correspondence.” Using the Fenchel–Rockafellar system of conjugacy correspondence, we define a transformation ${}^ * {\text{e}}$ and proceed to transform Problem (P), the original problem, into $({\text{P}}_{\text{e}}^ *  )$, the so-called- envelope problem. The derivation of $({\text{P}}_{\text{e}}^ *  )$ from (P) exploits convexity rather than differentiability of the system error norm. The solvability of $({\text{P}}_{\text{e}}^ *  )$ is then considered. To meet this end, we develop a subdifferential description of the dual objective function associated with $({\text{P}}_{\text{e}}^ *  )$. When the output error norm is polyhedral, it is shown how one can get a practical hold on $({\text{P}}_{\text{e}}^ *  )$, i.e., a linear programming approach becomes feasible for computation of approximate numerical solutions.
