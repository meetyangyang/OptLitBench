Optimal Boundary Control of Nonlinear Hyperbolic Conservation Laws with Switched Boundary Data
Sebastian  Pfaff and Stefan  Ulbrich;
Abstract
We consider the optimal control of initial-boundary value problems for entropy solutions of scalar hyperbolic conservation laws. In particular, we consider initial-boundary value problems where the initial and boundary data switch between different $C^1$-functions at certain switching points and both the functions and the switching points are controlled. We show that the control-to-state mapping is differentiable in a certain generalized sense, which implies Fréchet-differentiability with respect to the control functions and the switching points for the composition with a tracking type functional, even in the presence of shocks. We also present an adjoint-based formula for the gradient of the reduced objective functional.
Keywords
