A Behavioral Approach to Time-Varying Linear Systems. Part 2: Descriptor Systems
Achim  Ilchmann and Volker  Mehrmann;
Abstract
In the sequel to [A. Ilchmann and V. Mehrmann, {\em SIAM J.\ Control Optim.}, 44 (2005), pp.\ 1725--1747], we discuss a behavioral approach for linear, time-varying, differential algebraic (descriptor) systems with real analytic coefficients. The analysis is ``almost global" in the sense that the analysis is not restricted to an interval $\I\subset \R$ but is allowed for the ``time axis" $\R\!\setminus\!\T$, where $\T$ is a discrete set of critical points, at which the solution may exhibit a finite escape time. Controllable, observable, and autonomous behavior for linear time-varying descriptor systems is characterized.
Keywords
