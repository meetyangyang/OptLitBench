Uniform Convergence of the Potential Function Algorithm
Lloyd  Fisher and S. J.  Yakowitz;
Abstract
The identification problem of concern here is the estimation of a real function $f(x)$ by means of noisy observations $\{ (X_i ,f(X_i ) + \eta _i (X_i ))\} $ of its pairs, the $X_i $’s being chosen independently according to some fixed law P. The approach taken for estimation is the “potential function” method (its sources are referenced herein), to wit: Choose $f_0 $ arbitrarily and define the sequence $\{ f_n \} $ by the recursive relation $f_{n + 1} (x) = f_n (x) + \gamma _n (f(X_{n + 1} ) + \eta (X_{n + 1} ) - f_n (X_{n + 1} ))K(X_{n + 1} ,x)$, K being a positive symmetric kernel. From earlier publications it is known that under certain mild restrictions $E[\| {f_n  - f} \|^2 ] \to 0$ in the $L_2 (p)$-norm. Rates of convergence have been obtained in the restrictive case that $K(x,y) = \sum_{i - 1}^N {\lambda _i^2 } \phi _i (x)\phi _i (y)$ and $f(x) \in {\operatorname{span}}\{ \phi _i ,1 \leqq i \leqq N\} $. The contribution of this paper is to prove that while no uniform bounds exist in the $L_2 (p)$-norm (we prove this) if $\{ \phi _i \} $ is an infinite set, we do have $E[\| {f - f_n } \|_k ^2 ] < C_n (\| f \|)$ for the norm $\| g \|_k^2  = \int {\int g (x)g(y)K(x,y)p(x)p(y)dxdy} $ and $\{ C_n (r)\} $ a sequence converging to 0 for each positive r. A final result concerns the rate at which increasing finite-dimensional projections of $f_n  - f$ converge to 0 in the $L_2 (p)$-norm. From our methods it is seen that if $f \notin V = {\operatorname{span}}(\{ \phi _i \} )$, then $f_n $ converges in the mean to the projection of f on V.
