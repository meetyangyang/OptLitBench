Boundary Controllability of Hyperbolic Partial Differential Equations
Tunc  Geveci;
Abstract
It is shown that for a large class of first-order hyperbolic systems with constant coefficients, including strictly hyperbolic or symmetric systems, and where the boundary conditions result in well-posed mixed initial-boundary value problems, any initial state $u_0  \in H^1 (\Omega )$ can be steered by boundary control to any final state $u_T  \in H^1 (\Omega )$ at any time $T > T_0 $ (where \[\Omega  \subset \mathbb{R}^n \], $n \geqq 2$ is a bounded domain with smooth boundary and $T_0 $ depends on the system and on $\Omega $) provided that all rays escape to infinity.
