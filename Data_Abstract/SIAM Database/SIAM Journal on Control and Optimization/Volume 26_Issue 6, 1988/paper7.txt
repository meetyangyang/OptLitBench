Realization and Canonicity for Implicit Systems
José  Grimm;
Abstract
This paper studies realization theory and canonicity conditions for implicit systems. Here an implicit system is any system defined by an input-output finite-dimensional, linear, time-invariant relation, in discrete or continuous time (they are also called “generalized” or “descriptor” systems in the literature).The familiar definitions of canonicity and minimality are extended to such systems, and a so-called “reduced form” proves that, as for classical systems, minimality and canonicity are two equivalent concepts. We replace the notion of transfer function by a notion of external form, and prove that minimal realization can always be achieved.
