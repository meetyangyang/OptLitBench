On Characterizing Optimal Policies in Infinite-Horizon Stochastic Control
D. P.  Kennedy;
Abstract
An infinite-horizon sequential-decision model in discrete time is studied. Throughout, the minimal conditional expected loss at time n is defined as the essential infimum of the conditional expected losses at n associated with those policies that may be used after n. Necessary and sufficient conditions for conserving policies to be optimal are developed. An outline of how the model extends to continuous time is given, and the corresponding characterization of optimal conserving policies in continuous time is presented.
