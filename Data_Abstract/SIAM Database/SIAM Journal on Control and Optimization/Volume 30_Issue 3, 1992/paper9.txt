Minimal Length Curves That Are Not Embeddable in an Open Planar Set: The Problem of a Lost Swimmer with a Compass
R.  Hassin and A.  Tamir;
Abstract
Given an open bounded set S in $R^2 $, the problem of computing a path f of minimum size such that for every $x \in S$ the set $\{ x\}  + f$ intersects the boundary of S is considered. The existence of such paths is proved both when the path size is its length and when it is its (one-dimensional Hausdorff outer) measure. Some theorems characterizing optimal paths are proved and it is shown that when S is convex, the minimum width chords of ${\operatorname{CI}}(S)$ are optimal with respect to both size definitions.
Keywords
