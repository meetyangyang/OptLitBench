Criteria of Stabilizability for Switching-Control Systems with Solvable Linear Approximations
Xiongping  Dai;
Abstract
We study the stability and stabilizability of a continuous-time switched control system that consists of the time-invariant $n$-dimensional subsystems $\dot{x}=A_ix+B_i(x)u$ $(x\in\mathbb{R}^n, t\in\mathbb{R}_+,\textrm{ and }u\in\mathbb{R}^{m_i})$, where $i\in\{1,\dotsc,N\}$ and a switching signal $\sigma({\boldsymbol\cdot})\colon\mathbb{R}_+\rightarrow\{1,\dotsc,N\}$ which orchestrates switching between these subsystems above, where $A_i\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}, n\ge1, N\ge2, m_i\ge1$, and where $B_i({\boldsymbol\cdot})\colon\mathbb{R}^n\rightarrow\mathbb{R}^{n\times m_i}$ satisfies the condition $\|B_i(x)\|\le{\boldsymbol\beta}\|x\|$ for all $x\in\mathbb{R}^n$. We show that, if $\{A_1,\dotsc,A_N\}$ generates a solvable Lie algebra over the field $\mathbb{C}$ of complex numbers and there exists an element ${\boldsymbol A}$ in the convex hull $\mathrm{co}\{A_1,\dotsc,A_N\}$ in $\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$ such that the affine system $\dot{x}={\boldsymbol A} x$ is exponentially stable, then there is a constant ${\boldsymbol \delta}>0$ for which one can design „sufficiently many” piecewise-constant switching signals $\sigma(t)$ so that the switching-control systems $\dot{x}(t)=A_{\sigma(t)}x(t)+B_{\sigma(t)}(x(t))u(t)$, $x(0)\in\mathbb{R}^n\textrm{ and } t\in\mathbb{R}_+$, are globally exponentially stable for any measurable external inputs $u(t)\in\mathbb{R}^{m_{\sigma(t)}}$ with $\|u(t)\|\le\boldsymbol \delta$.
