Classical and Strong Convexity of Sublevel Sets and Application to Attainable Sets of Nonlinear Systems
Alexander  Weber and Gunther  Reissig;
Abstract
Necessary and sufficient conditions for convexity and strong convexity, respectively, of connected sublevel sets that are defined by finitely many real-valued $C^{1,1}$-maps are presented. A novel characterization of strongly convex sets in terms of the so-called local quadratic support is proved. The results concerning strong convexity are used to derive sufficient conditions for attainable sets of continuous-time nonlinear systems to be strongly convex. An application of these conditions is a novel method to overapproximate attainable sets when strong convexity is present.
Keywords
sublevel set, convexity, strong convexity, local quadratic support, attainable set, Primary, 52A30;  Secondary, 52A20, 93C10, 93C15,
