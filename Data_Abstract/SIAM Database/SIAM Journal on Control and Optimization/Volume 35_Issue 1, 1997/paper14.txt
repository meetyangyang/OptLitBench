Behavioral Controllability of Delay-Differential Systems
Paula  Rocha and Jan C.  Willems;
Abstract
In this paper we will prove that the system described by the delay-differential equation $R(d/dt,\Delta)w=0$ (with $\Delta$ the unit delay operator) is controllable if and only if the rank of $R(\lambda,e^{-\lambda})$ is constant for all $\lambda \in {\bkC}$. This condition is compared with the existing results obtained both by the analytic approach and by the algebraic approach to delay-differential systems.
Keywords
behavioral systems, controllability, delay-differential systems, polynomial matrices, 93B05,
