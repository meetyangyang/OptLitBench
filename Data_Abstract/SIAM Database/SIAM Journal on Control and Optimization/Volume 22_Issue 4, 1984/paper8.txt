Optimal Interpolation for Linear Stochastic Systems
Michele  Pavon;
Abstract
Let y, satisfying $dy = Cxdt + Ddw$, $y(0) = 0$, represent the noisy observation of the Gauss–Markov process x generated by $dx = Axdt + Bdw$, $x(0) = x_0 $, on the finite interval $[0,T]$. In this paper we solve the following interpolation problem: determine the best least-squares estimate of $x(t)$, $t \in [0,T]$, given the increments of y on the intervals $[0,T_1 ]$ and $[T_2,T ]$, where $0 < T_1  < T_2  < T$, and the statistics of $x_0 $. Several alternative expressions for the optimal interpolator are derived, some of which are phrased in terms of two Kalman-Bucy estimates. As a by-product, we also solve the interpolation problem for the missing increments of y.
Keywords
nonstationary interpolation, Kalman–Bucy model, stochastic realization theory,
