Turnpike Property of Optimal Solutions of Infinite-Horizon Variational Problems
A. J.  Zaslavski;
Abstract
Given $x_0 \in R^n$ we study the infinite-horizon problem of minimizing the expression $\int^T_0 f(x(t), x'(t))dt$ as T grows to infinity, where a function x: $[0, \infty) \rightarrow R^n$ is absolutely continuous and satisfies the initial condition $x(0) = x_0$ and $f = f(x, u)$ is an integrand. We study the structure of $(f)$-weakly optimal solutions and establish the turnpike property for a generic integrand f.
Keywords
infinite horizon, weakly optimal function, good function, turnpike property, 49J99,
