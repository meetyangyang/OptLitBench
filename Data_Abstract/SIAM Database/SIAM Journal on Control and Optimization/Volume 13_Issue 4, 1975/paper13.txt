An Example of a Continuous Junction for a Singular Control Problem of Even Order
Helmut  Maurer;
Abstract
The junction theorem of McDanell and Powers [1] gives a characterization of the junction between optimal singular and nonsingular subarcs. The theorem states in particular that for a singular control problem of even order an optimal piecewise analytic control must be continuous. No example for such a continuous junction seems to be known in the literature. In this paper we give for every even order q an example for which optimality can be proved using standard sufficiency theorems.
