Global $(f,g)$-Invariance of Nonlinear Systems
W.  Dayawansa, D.  Cheng, W. M.  Boothby, and T. J.  Tarn;
Abstract
Consider a nonlinear system $\dot x = f(x) + \sum _{j = 1}^m g_j (x)u_j $ and a nonsingular involutive distribution $\Delta $ defined on a state space M which is a $C^\infty  $ manifold. The authors investigate the existence of nonlinear feedback $(\alpha ,\beta )$ such that $[f + g\alpha ,\Delta ] \subset \Delta $ and $[g\beta ,\Delta ] \subset \Delta $, i.e., $(f,g)$-invariance. A geometric interretation of weak $(f,g)$-invariance, i.e., $[f,\Delta ]$, $[G,\Delta ] \subset \Delta  + G$ is given in terms of a transverse $GL(q,R)$-structure to the codimension q foliation $\mathcal{q}$ defined by $\Delta $. This interpretation allows a simplified proof of the Quaker Lemma—proved by other means in [15], [16] and [21]—and helps to somewhat clarify the global picture. As an application the case $M = S^3 $ is considered in detail. The introduction contains a discussion of the relation of this work to other studies of global $(f,g)$-invariance and to relevant foliation theory literature. Resolution of this problem is important for extension of results using the concept of $(A,B)$-invariant subspaces to nonlinear systems.
Keywords
