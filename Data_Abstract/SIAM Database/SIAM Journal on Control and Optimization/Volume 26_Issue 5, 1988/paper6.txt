An Evasion Game with Barriers
V. J.  Baston and F. A.  Bostock;
Abstract
This paper considers the following two-person zero-sum multistage game. An evader starts at some given point $A_s $ in the ordered set of points $A_0 ,A_1 , \cdots ,A_n $ and at discrete intervals of time $t = 1,2, \cdots $ chooses to move to one of the points adjacent to him or stay where he is. A gunner with a single bullet may at each of the same discrete intervals of time either fire the bullet at any of the points $A_r $ or hold his fire. The gunner always hits the point at which he aims and the bullet takes one unit of time to reach its target. The payoff to the gunner is 1 if he hits the evader, $\mu $ (where $|\mu | < 1$) if he fires and misses, and 0 if he never fires. It is shown that, whatever point $A_s $ the evader starts at, the value of the game is ${{(1 + \mu )} / 2}$.
