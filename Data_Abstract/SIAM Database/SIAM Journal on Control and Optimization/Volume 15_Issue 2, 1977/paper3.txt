Differential Games with No Information
D. J.  Wilson;
Abstract
This paper considers a broad class of differential games of prescribed duration in which the players obtain no information about the state variables. It is shown that such games always have a value when the players can choose their controls by means of a mixed strategy.It is further shown that a player can always implement a mixed strategy by drawing a random number from the uniform distribution on the unit interval and then using a control which is determined by the number so chosen.
