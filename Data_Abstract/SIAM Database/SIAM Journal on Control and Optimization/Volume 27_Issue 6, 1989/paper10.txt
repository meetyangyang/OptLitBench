A Matroid-Theoretic Approach to Structurally Fixed Modes of Control Systems
Kazuo  Murota;
Abstract
The structurally fixed modes for a decentralized control system described in the descriptor form $F\dot {\bf x} = A{\bf x} + B{\bf u}$, $H{\bf y} = C{\bf x}$ are investigated under a physically reasonable assumption that the coefficients in the equations are classified into independent physical parameters and dimensionless fixed constants. A necessary and sufficient condition for the existence of structurally fixed modes is given in matroid-theoretic terms; the condition can be tested by an efficient algorithm for the independent-flow problem. The combinatorial canonical form of a layered mixed matrix plays a central role in deriving the condition.
