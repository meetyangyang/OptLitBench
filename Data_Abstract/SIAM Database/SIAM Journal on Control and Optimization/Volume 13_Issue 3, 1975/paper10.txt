Relaxed Control Problems with State Equality Constraints
A. B.  Schwarzkopf;
Abstract
This paper derives a set of necessary conditions for a general optimal control problem with bilateral state and endpoint constraints with no assumptions of normality or regularity involving the constraints. The basic result is a separation theorem in a linear topological space and leads to the Pontryagin maximum principle. Although this separation theorem involves functionals from the dual of $L^\infty  $, we show that, under weaker hypotheses than generally assumed, this result leads to the classical maximum principle. The extension of these results to include unilateral constraints is indicated.
