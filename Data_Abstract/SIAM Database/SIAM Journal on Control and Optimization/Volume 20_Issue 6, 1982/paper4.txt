Approximating a Second-Order Directional Derivative for Nonsmooth Convex Functions
J. B.  Hiriart-Urruty;
Abstract
For a lower-semicontinuous convex function f, the approximate second-order directional derivative $(d,\delta ) \mapsto f''_\varepsilon  (x_0 ;d,\delta )$ is defined through the $\varepsilon $-directional derivative $f'_\varepsilon  (x;d)$. The function $v_d :x \mapsto v_d (x) = f'_\varepsilon  (x;d)$ is, for all $\varepsilon  > 0$, locally Lipschitz on inf (domf) and, at those points where it is not differentiable, $v_d$ admits a directional derivative $v'_d (x_0 ;\delta )$ for all $\delta $, which we precisely denote by $f''_\varepsilon  (x_0 ;d,\delta )$. The objective of the present work is two-fold: to classify all the possible differentiability properties of $v_d$ according to the behavior of the function $\lambda  \mapsto f(x_0  + \lambda d)$ on $R_ +  $, and to study the existence or nonexistence of the limit of $f''_\varepsilon  (x_0 ;d,\delta )$ when $\varepsilon  \to 0^ +  $.
