The “Bang-Bang” Problem for Certain Control Systems in $GL(n,R)$
Hector J.  Sussmann;
Abstract
We discuss the linear control problem $X(t) = [A_0 (t) + \sum_{i = 1}^m {u_i (t)A_i (t)} ]X(t)$, where $A_0 , \cdots ,A_m $ are $n \times n$ matrix-valued functions of time, and where $X(t) \in GL(n,R)$. We show that the set attainable from any element $M \in GL(n,R)$ at time t by “bang-bang” controls is closed, provided the following very strong assumption is satisfied: for all i, j and for all $t'$, $t''$ such that $0 \leqq t' \leqq t$, $0 \leqq t'' \leqq t$, the matrices $A_i (t')$ and $A_j (t'')$ commute. We also show, by means of counterexamples, that these assumptions cannot be weakened.
