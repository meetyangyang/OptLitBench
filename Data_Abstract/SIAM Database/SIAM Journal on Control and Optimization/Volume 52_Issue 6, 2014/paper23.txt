Controllability of the Linear One-dimensional Wave Equation with Inner Moving Forces
Carlos  Castro, Nicolae  Cîndea, and Arnaud  Münch;
Abstract
We address the null inner controllability of the one-dimensional wave equation, with supports of the controls that may vary with respect to the time variable. We consider theoretical and numerical aspects. Assuming a geometric optic condition on the support of the controls, we first prove a generalized observability inequality for the homogeneous wave equation implying the controllability. We then introduce and prove the well-posedness of a mixed formulation that characterizes the controls of minimal square integrable norm. A numerical approximation of this formulation, based on a conformal space-time finite element method, is then presented. Several numerical experiments are finally reported.
Keywords
linear wave equation, null controllability, mixed formulations, finite element approximation, Primary, 35L10, 65M12; Secondary, 93B40,
