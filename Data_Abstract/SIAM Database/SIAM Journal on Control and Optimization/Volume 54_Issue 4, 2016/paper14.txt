Exact Controllability of Structural Acoustic Interactions with Variable Coefficients
Yuxiang  Liu, Bandar  Bin-Mohsin, Hichem  Hajaiej, Peng-Fei  Yao, and Goong  Chen;
Abstract
This paper studies exact controllability properties of two coupled wave equations with variable coefficients by a Riemannian geometrical approach. One of the PDEs holds on the interior of a bounded open domain $\Omega$ in $\mathbb{R}^m$ and the other on a piece $\Gamma_0$ of the boundary $\partial\Omega$. First, an exact  controllability result is established by assuming the existence of an escape vector field with two boundary controls, the first of which is a Neumann control  and the second a distributed control for the boundary equation. Moreover, a geometric structure between the boundary and the variable-coefficient principal part  is considered in order to implement one control on the boundary segment $\Gamma_0$ only. Concrete examples are given for such geometrical configurations.
