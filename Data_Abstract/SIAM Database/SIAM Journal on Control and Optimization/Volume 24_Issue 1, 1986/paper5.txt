On Seminormality of Integral Functionals and Their Integrands
E. J.  Balder;
Abstract
We present a definition of seminormality which extends classical notions due to Tonelli [Fondamenti di Calcolo delle Variazioni, 1921], McShane [Ann Scuola Norm. Sup. Pisa (2), 3 (1934), pp. 181–211, 287–315] and Cesari [Traps. Amer. Math. Soc., 124 (1966), pp. 369–412; J. Optim. Theory Appl., 6 (1970), pp. 114–137; SIAM J. Control Optim., 9 (1971), pp. 287–315], and applies to both integrands (“seminormality in the small”) and their integral functionals (“seminormality in the large”). Our main result states that under very general hypotheses seminormality in the small and large are equivalent. By introducing a notion called Nagumo tightness, we show that the usual sufficient conditions for the lower semicontinuity of an integral functional also imply a form of seminormality of the integral functional. Necessary conditions for the lower semicontinuity of an integral functional can also be obtained from our results.
