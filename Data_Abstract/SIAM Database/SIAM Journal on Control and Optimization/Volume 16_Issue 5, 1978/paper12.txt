Controllability of Nondifferentiable Hereditary Processes
J.  Warga;
Abstract
We derive a “controllability-multiplier rule” alternative for control problems defined by hereditary functional-integral equations of the form \[y(t) = \int {f(t,\tau ,\xi (y)(\tau ),u(\tau ))\mu (d\tau )} \quad (t \in T)\] and by associated “isoperimetric” and unilateral constraints. Our assumptions are weaker than customary because $f(t,\tau , \cdot ,u)$ and the functions of state variables that define the constraints and the cost functional are assumed to be Lipschitz-continuous but not necessarily differentiable. The arguments are based on a controllability model of a general nondifferentiable optimization problem developed in a companion paper.
