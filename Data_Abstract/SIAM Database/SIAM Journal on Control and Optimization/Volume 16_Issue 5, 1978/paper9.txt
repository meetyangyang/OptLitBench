Functionals Defined on Functions of Bounded Variation in $\mathbb{R}^n $ and the Lebesgue Area
Francesco  Ferro;
Abstract
Let ${\text{BV}}_b (\mathbb{R}^n )$ be the space of all functions T such that $T \in L_{{\text{loc}}}^1 (\mathbb{R}^n )$ and $\nabla T$ is a vector-valued measure of finite total variation on $\mathbb{R}^n $. In [1] we considered the functional \[J_{\tilde L} (T) = \min \left\{ {\mathop {\lim \inf }\limits_\alpha  \int_\Omega  {L\left( {x,u_\alpha  (x),\nabla u_\alpha  (x)} \right)dx} :u_\alpha  \xrightarrow{{w^ *  }}T} \right\},\quad T \in {\text{BV}}_b (\mathbb{R}^n ),\] where L is a proper normal integrand; $u_\alpha   \in L_{{\text{loc}}}^1 (\mathbb{R}^n )$, $\nabla u_\alpha   \in (L^1 (\mathbb{R}^n ))^n $ and $u_\alpha  \xrightarrow{{w^ *  }}T$ if and only if $\int_\Omega  {u_\alpha  }  \to \int_\Omega  T $ and $\int_{\mathbb{R}^n } {G\nabla u_\alpha  }  \to \int_{\mathbb{R}^n } {G\nabla T} $ for every continuous vector-valued function G with compact support in $\mathbb{R}^n $. In this paper we give some results about the $w^ *  $ topology defined on ${\text{BV}}_b (\mathbb{R}^n )$ and derive new properties of the functional $J_{\tilde L} $. Afterward we carry on a comparison with the functional defining the Lebesgue area of a nonparametric surface.
