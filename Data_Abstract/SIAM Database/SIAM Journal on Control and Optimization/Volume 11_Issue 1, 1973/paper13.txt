Here, we consider optimization problems with several constraints, i.e., we seek to minimize a functional J on a closed, convex set K in a real, reflexive Banach space, K being defined in terms of q functionals $G_i$, $i = 1, \cdots ,q$: \[K = \{ v \in V|G_i (v) \leqq 0,\quad i = 1,2, \cdots ,q\} .\] To arrive at the solution u, we consider \[J_{r(h)} (v_h ) = J(v_h ) + \frac{1}{{r(h)}}\sum_{i = 1}^q {H_i^2 (v_h )} ,\] where \[H_i (v_h ) = \left\{ \begin{gathered}  0\quad {\text{if}}\quad G_i (v_h ) \geqq 0, \hfill \\  G_i (v_h )\quad {\text{if}}\quad G_i (v_h ) > 0, \hfill \\ \end{gathered}  \right.\qquad i = 1, \cdots ,q,\] and $v_h  \in V_h $ a finite-dimensional space satisfying $ \cup _{h > 0} V_h $ is dense in V. By applying the method of local variations to $J_{r(h)} (v_h )$, we demonstrate the strong convergence of the procedure as in [18]. Finally, in the case of functions of N variables, we give error estimates and show that the convergence can be of the order of r.
