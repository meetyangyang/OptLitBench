An Existence Theorem for Lagrange Problems with Unbounded Controls and a Slender Set of Exceptional Points
L.  Cesari, J. R.  La Palm, and D. A.  Sanchez;
Abstract
In a number of existence theorems for Lagrange problems with unbounded controls certain growth conditions are required, which generally can be omitted for bounded controls. In the present paper the authors show that even for unbounded controls the growth conditions can be dispensed with in an arbitrary subset in the $tx$-space $E_{1 + n} $, provided such a subset is “slender” according to suitable definitions. Any set contained in finitely many or countably many smooth curves $x = \phi (t)$, $a \leqq t \leqq b$, is certainly slender. Many examples are given.
