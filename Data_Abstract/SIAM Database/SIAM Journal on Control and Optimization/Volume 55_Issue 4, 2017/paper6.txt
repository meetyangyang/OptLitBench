Optimal Control of Non-Smooth Hyperbolic Evolution Maxwell Equations in Type-II Superconductivity
Irwin  Yousept;
Abstract
We analyze the optimal control of an  electromagnetic process in type-II superconductivity.  The PDE-constrained optimization problem is to find an optimal applied current density, which steers the electromagnetic fields to the desired ones in the presence of a type-II superconductor. The governing PDE system for the electromagnetic fields consists of hyperbolic evolution Maxwell equations with a nonlinear and nonsmooth constitutive  law for the electric field and the current density based on the Bean critical-state model.  Through the use of the Maxwell theory, the semigroup theory, Helmholtz decomposition, and results on maximal monotone operators,   we develop a    mathematical theory  including an existence analysis and first-order necessary optimality conditions for the nonsmooth PDE-constrained optimization problem.
