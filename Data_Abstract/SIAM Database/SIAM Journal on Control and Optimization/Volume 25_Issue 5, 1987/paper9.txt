Reaching Zero Rapidly
Steven  Orey, Victor  Pestien, and William  Sudderth;
Abstract
The controlled process $X(t)$ with values in $( { - \infty ,0} ]$ is given by a stochastic differential equation: \[dX(t) = \mu (t)dt + \sigma (t)dW_t ,\quad X(0) = x\] where the nonanticipative controls $\mu $ and $\sigma $ are to be chosen so that $(\mu (t),\sigma (t))$ remains in a given set $\mathcal{S}$. The object is to maximize (minimize) the expectation of $\beta ^T $ where $0 < \beta  < 1$$(\beta  > 1)$ and T is the hitting time of zero. A complete solution is given for any $\mathcal{S}$, and an application is made, to continuous-time red-and-black.
