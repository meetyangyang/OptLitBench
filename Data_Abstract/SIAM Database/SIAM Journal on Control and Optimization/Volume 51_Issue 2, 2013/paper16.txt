Stabilization of Well-Posed Infinite-Dimensional Systems by Dynamic Sampled-Data Feedback
H.  Logemann;
Abstract
It is shown that a set of six natural conditions is necessary and sufficient for the existence of a finite-dimensional stabilizing sampled-data controller for a well-posed infinite-dimensional system. The underlying stability concept for the sampled-data system is reminiscent of the notion of input-to-state stability from nonlinear control theory.
