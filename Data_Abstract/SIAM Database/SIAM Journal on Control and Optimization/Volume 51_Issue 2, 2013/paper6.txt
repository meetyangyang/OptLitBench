On a Certainty Equivalence Design of Continuous-Time Stochastic Systems
Uwe  Küchler and Vyacheslav A.  Vasiliev;
Abstract
This paper presents a certainty equivalence design method with application for continuous-time stochastic systems with unknown parameters. The first aim is to approximate the observed process $x=(x_t)_{t\geq0},$ satisfying a stochastic differential equation with control, by choosing a control process $(u_t)_{t\geq0}$ to a stable Ornstein--Uhlenbeck process $(x^0_t)_{t\geq0},$ driven by the same Wiener process. Based on observation of $x$ for a given $\varepsilon>0$ an adaptive control process $u^\varepsilon=(u^\varepsilon_t)_{t\geq0}$ is constructed such that the corresponding observed process $x(u^\varepsilon)=(x^\varepsilon_t)_{t\geq0}$  satisfies the relation $ \sup\nolimits_{t\geq t_\varepsilon}\ E(x^\varepsilon_t-x^0_t)^2\leq \varepsilon$. The time $t_\varepsilon$ $\mbox{(}\varepsilon$ is a threshold quantity) is found. A similar problem is solved for a stochastic delay differential equation with an unknown parameter. It is shown that the time $t_\varepsilon$ has equal rates of increase $\varepsilon^{-1}\ln \varepsilon^{-1}$ in both problems. Moreover, a target inequality, which ensures the stability of the controlled processes in the sense of the $L_2$-norm, is established. Similar problems for more general controlled  multidimensional systems can be solved using sequential estimators of unknown parameters.
Keywords
