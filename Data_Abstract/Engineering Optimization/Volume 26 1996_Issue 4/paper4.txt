GEOMETRICALLY CONSTRAINED THICKNESS OPTIMIZATION OF FLEXIBLE BEAMS
 P. D.   GOSLING ; B.   ROUSSELET ;
Abstract
 An energy formulation which defines the equilibrium position of the neutral axis of a laterally loaded beam is summarised. Geometric non-linearity is introduced through an inextensibility constraint, nonlinear in the unknown configuration. Several sensitivity formulations are developed and investigated. Using a decomposed from of the inextensibility constraint sensitivity analyses are performed. Assuming a direct differentiation method, solutions to thickness sensitivities are presented. It is demonstrated that satisfaction of the constraints describing the geometric non-linearity is critical if accurate sensitivities are to be obtained. Several algorithms are proposed which aim to provide unconditionally accurate sensitivities using the proposed formulation. Optimized solutions to cantilever, propped cantilever, and arch problems are given. 
Keywords
 Structural optimization, geometric non-linearity, inextensibility, thickness sensitivity, beams
