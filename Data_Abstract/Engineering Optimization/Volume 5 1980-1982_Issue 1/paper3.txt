A DYNAMIC MODEL FOR OPTIMAL SEGMENTATION OF WALLS BUILT ON NON-LINEAR SLOPES
 SHAUL P.   LADANY ;
Abstract
 Walls on non-linear slopes are often built of horizontal segments each requiring the digging of a trench in which the depth increases with the length of the segment along an incline, while the effective height of the wall above ground decreases. A set of optimal segment lengths is obtained by minimizing total cost consisting of the fixed cost per segment, cost of digging a trench for each segment and cost of building the segment to reach a required effective height. A dynamic generalized cost model is developed together with a discrete approximation method which simplifies compulations.
