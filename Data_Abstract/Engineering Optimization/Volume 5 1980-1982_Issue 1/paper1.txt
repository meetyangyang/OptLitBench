A GEOMETRICAL PROCEDURE TO DESIGN TRUSSES IN A GIVEN AREA
 J-M.   LAGACHE ;
Abstract
 Whilst researching optimal design of trusses lying in a given convex region, 10 results were obtained which did not accord with our basic knowledge about Michell theory (Ref. 1-8), Briefly speaking and from a practical standpoint, the optimal frames had a finite number of non-orthogonal members either in tension or compression, were neither Maxwell nor Michell frameworks and were not, of course, trivial solutions which could have been devised at a. glance. The practical determination of those frames is here completely reviewed and the analytical method described in Ref. 10 turns into a much more attractive and efficient geometrical procedure. Moreover, that procedure applies to a wide range of non-convex problems and gives almost optimal frames us well as a measurement of their efficiency.
