FORMULATIONS OF RELIABILITY-ORIENTED OPTIMIZATION
 R.   RACKWITZ ; R.   CUNTZE ;
Abstract
 Modern first-order and second-order reliability methods are reviewed with respect to optimization of structural systems with reliability as either a constraint or as the objective. It is demonstrated that these reliability methods by themselves involve non-linear mathematical programming provided that an adequate formulation of the reliability problem is possible. The methods described are particularly suited for highly reliable structures, and also produce gradient information as a side result so that efficient optimization methods can be applied. The concepts are illustrated by a simple example. 
Keywords
Structures, system reliability, first-order reliability method, second-order reliability method, safety index.
