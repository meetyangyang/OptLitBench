QUALITATIVE MONOTONICITY ANALYSIS OF A PROBLEM INVOLVING DIFFERENTIAL EQUATIONS
 PANOS   PAPALAMBROS ;
Abstract
 Monotonicity analysis is applied to an optimum design problem where the constraints are expressed by differential equations and their associated boundary conditions. The qualitative analysis does not utilize explicit or numerical solutions. Instead, the behavior of the equations is determined qualitatively with the aid of maximum principles. Equivalent functional constraints are derived containing information about the monotonic behavior of the design variables. This information is used to determine constraint activity. The results have generality and physical interpretation and may be used subsequently for efficient numerical implementation. The application problem examines the design of a small reflected-core nuclear reactor studied originally as a nonlinear programming problem.
