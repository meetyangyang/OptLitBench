A novel technique of shape optimization for the contact parts of bolt–nut fastening structures
 Jing   Han *  ; Koetsu   Yamazaki ;
Abstract
 This article deals with the shape optimization of the contact part of a bolt–nut fastening structure using a novel technique. In this method, all contact parts are composed as a quasi-continuum structure using a kind of thin film element. The Young modulus of the thin film elements depends on the compressive and tensile states, and is much smaller than that of usual elements. The shape optimization problem of the quasi-continuum structure is then solved by the structural analysis and the optimization software GENESIS. Numerical results of the shape optimization of the bolt-nut fastening structure are given to prove the effectiveness of the new technique. The proposed technique is easy to apply without any additional computational time and code for complex contact analysis.  
Keywords
Optimum design, Contact problem, Stress concentration, Finite element method, Shape optimization
