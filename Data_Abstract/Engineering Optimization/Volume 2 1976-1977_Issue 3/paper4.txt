APPLICATION OF THE PERTURBATION METHOD TO OPTIMAL SHUTTLE REENTRY TRAJECTORIS†
 W. E.   WILLIAMSON   Assistant Professor ; R. W.   GREENE   Principal Systems Analyst ; D. G.   HULL   Associate Professor ;
Abstract
 The applicability of the perturbation or shooting method to the computation of optimal shuttle reentry trajectories. of long duration (1800 sec) is established. The example problem is that of minimizing the heat load for a given crossrange subject to inequality constraints on the angle of attack and the roll angle. The physical model used is approximate, but it contains the relevant features of shuttle reentry. All elements of the perturbation method-integration of differential equations, matrix inversion, and corner-point calculation-are examined to ensure a minimum level of accuracy. Initial Lagrange multipliers are originally obtained by selecting control boundaries so that nearly constant controls are used. Then, the process of gradually relaxing the boundaries and optimizing the trajectory is followed until the prescribed control limits, and hence, the desired optimal trajectory are obtained.
