OPTIMAL SHOVEL-TRUCK OPERATIONS
 DAVID G.   CARMICHAEL ;
Abstract
 Charts are presented as the solution of the optimal shovel-truck configuration problem. In particular, for given parameters defining the shovel-truck operation (namely average haul, load and manoeuvre times), the charts enable the determination of the optimal truck fleet size. The objective function is one of cost per unit production of the operation. The model of the operation is based on finite source queueing theory results. Several queueing models are outlined and the sensitivity of the optimum solution to the assumed model is examined. Time and production constraints may be included with little extra work, The charts provide an effective and quick means for establishing the optimum truck fleet size in any earthmoving, quarrying or open-cut mining operation of the shovel-truck type. 
Keywords
Earthmoving operations, truck fleet size, optimization, queueing theory, sensitivity analysis
