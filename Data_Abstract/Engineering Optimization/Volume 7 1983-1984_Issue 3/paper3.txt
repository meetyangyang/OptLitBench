ANALYTICAL METHODS IN PLASTIC STRUCTURAL DESIGN
 J.   LELLEP ; Ü   LEPIK ;
Abstract
 Rapid progress in the optimal design of non-elastic structures has been taking place during the last three decades. In these thirty years a great number of investigations have been conducted which, according to Rozvany and Mroz228 “are directed at two aspects: application of numerical methods to discretized representations of practical design problems, and fundamental properties of optimal structures examined by means of analytical methods and relatively simple, idealized mathematical models.” Obviously, both kind of studies are important. Perhaps due to the recent popularity of digital techniques research into numerical methods has been comparatively more intense but the authors completely agree with the point of view of Prager192 who has emphasized that: “Because they use available resources in a most efficient manner, optimal designs are likely to have unexpected properties requiring special care in the formulation of problems of structural optimization.” These words are confirmed by a number of examples. Thus the role of analytical methods cannot be neglected because these lead, as a rule, directly to exact results. In the present paper an attempt is made to review the most important analytical methods applied in the optimal design of plastic structures. The direct variational methods, the Drucker-Shield theory and the cost-gradient method are distinguished in the present paper, although there is not strict boundaries between these. The paper consists of two parts. Plastic structures subjected to quasi-static loading are considered in the first part, whereas dynamic structures are discussed in the second part. Linear and non-linear mathematical programming methods, the dynamic programming technique, as well as the finite element method and other numerical approaches are outside the scope of the paper.
