A HYBRID KNOWLEDGE-BASED SYSTEM: CASE STUDY
 K. S.   HINDI ; Y. M.   HAMAM ;
Abstract
 The idea of hybrid knowledge-based systems is introduced. It is argued that these systems can play a significant role in engineering decision making, particularly in the planning, design, scheduling and operation of large-scale systems. Combining branch and bound search with a knowledge-based system to form one variant of hybrid knowledge-based systems is discussed. A case study of the planning of low voltage electricity distribution networks is presented. 
Keywords
Optimization, knowledge-based, hybrid, branch and bound
