A FINITE ELEMENT BASED APPROACH TO MULTI-OBJECTIVE STRUCTURAL OPTIMIZATION USING GOAL PROGRAMMING
 PETER   CHANG ; JOSSEF   PERL ;
Abstract
 This paper presents a general multi-objective approach to structural optimization. The proposed approach consists of formulating and solving a finite element model as a Goal Programming problem. The proposed approach allows one to assign priorities to the various design objectives and avoid the need to assign subjective weights. Furthermore, since the proposed approach formulates the model in terms of finite elements, it can be applied to any structure. Even more important than the capability of the proposed model to provide a single optimal solution to a structural optimization problem, is its trade-off analysis capabilities. The desirable levels of the design parameters in structural optimization should not be determined independently of their cost implications and/ or their effect on other design parameters. The trade-off analysis capabilities of the proposed model allow us to analyze these implications. Two examples are presented to illustrate the formulation process of the proposed model and its capabilities as a decisionmaking tool for structural optimization. It is shown that the trade-off curves produced by the proposed model can provide useful information on the cost implications of various design parameters as well as on the trade-offs that exist among them. 
Keywords
Structural optimization, multi-objective optimization, goal programming, trade-off analysis
