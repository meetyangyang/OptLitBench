BANG-BANG CONTROL AND OPTIMUM STRUCTURAL DESIGN
 DAVID G.   CARMICHAEL ;
Abstract
 Certain problems in optimum structural design reduce to a totally or partially linear objective function and system model. For structures described in terms of one spatial coordinate, the optimum solution may turn out to be bang-bang, in the sense that the structural geometry switches from one constraint boundary to another at given values of the spatial coordinate. Knowing that the optimum solution lies on a constraint boundary simplifies the optimization problem; in effect the problem reduces to one of finding the “switching points”. Bang-bang control is common in many control systems; this paper exploits the idea for structural optimization problems. 
Keywords
 structural optimization, optimum control, bang-bang control
