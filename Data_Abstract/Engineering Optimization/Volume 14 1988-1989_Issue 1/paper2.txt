ON MULTIMODAL PARAMETRICAL OPTIMIZATION OF ARCHES AGAINST PLANE AND SPATIAL BUCKLING
 BOGDAN   BOCHENEK ;
Abstract
 A plane, elastic circular arch with a rectangular cross-section is optimized against both plane and spatial buckling. The arch is assumed to consist of a given number of prismatic segments, the cross-sectional dimensions of which are treated as the design parameters. Two cases are discussed. In the first only the optimal cross-sectional area of each segment is sought—one design parameter. In the second both depth and width of each segment are considered as the two independent design parameters. The necessity of multimodal formulation of such an optimization problem is pointed out. The problem is solved using Pomryagin's maximum principle. Some numerical examples are presented. 
Keywords
 Structural optimization, arches, buckling
