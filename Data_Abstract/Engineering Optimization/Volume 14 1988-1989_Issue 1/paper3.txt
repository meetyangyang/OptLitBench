TWO DIMENSIONAL SHAPE OPTIMIZATION WITH APPLICATION TO THE PLATE HOLE PROBLEM
 TERRY R.   WALKER ; RAINER   HOFF ;
Abstract
 The finite element method has been used in conjunction with a quasi-Newton optimization algorithm to investigate two dimensional shape optimization. Specifically, the case or a cutout in a flat steel plate has been investigated for the case where the plate cannot be considered infinite when compared to the area of the cutout at its center. Optimum cutout results are presented for finite square plates loaded under various degrees of biaxial tension. The optimum finite plate solutions are found to be very nearly elliptical, with axis ratios somewhat less than those predicted by theoretical solutions for infinite plates under similar loading conditions. 
Keywords
structural optimization, shape optimization, plates, cutouts
