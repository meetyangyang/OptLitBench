A COMPARISON OF OPTIMIZATION METHODS FOR ENGINEERING SYSTEM OPTIMIZATION
 S.   ADIBHATLA ; D. C.   LEIGH ;
Abstract
 Eight optimization methods, representing six classes of methods, are compared using engineering systems which are not described in closed form, instead of traditional analytic test functions. The methods were compared on the basis of consistency, number of function evaluations, and complexity of use. Substantial differences were found amongst the various classes of methods. 
Keywords
Optimization methods, nonlinear programming, algorithmic comparisons, process simulation
