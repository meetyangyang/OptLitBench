THE OPTIMIZATION OF DIAGONALLY BRACED THERMOPLASTIC OPEN CHANNELS FOR TORSIONAL STIFFNESS
 P.   DUMPLETON ;
Abstract
 A theoretical solution has been developed to predict the torsional rigidity of cross braced open channels. This solution has been verified both experimentally and with finite elements. Values of the cross bracing angle and the depth to width ratio of the channel which give the optimum torsional stiffness/weight have been determined. When the ratio of the channel depth to width is fixed the angle of the cross bracing has been determined for (a) optimum torsional stiffness and (b) optimum torsional stiffness/weight. 
Keywords
