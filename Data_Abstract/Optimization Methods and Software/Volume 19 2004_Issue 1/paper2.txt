Non-Convex feasibility problems and proximal point methods 
 Jean-noël   Corvellec ; Sjur Didrik   Flåm ;
Abstract
 A feasibility problem typically amounts to locate at least one point which belongs to several closed subsets of a Euclidean space. Equivalently, one might seek a vector which minimizes several associated objectives. We develop a method to solve a class of such problems, allowing here non-convex sets or functions. The method uses proximal point iterations, possibly with Bregman distances. More general spaces than Euclidean are accommodated. We also briefly reconsider the classical, convex case. 
Keywords
Feasibility problems, Proximal point methods, Variational principle, Alternating projections, Bregman functions
