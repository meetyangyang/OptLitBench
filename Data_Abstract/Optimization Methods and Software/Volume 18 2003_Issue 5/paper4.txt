On Conjugate Gradient Algorithms as Objects of Scientific Study
 J.L.   Nazareth ;
Abstract
 A family of conjugate gradient (CG) algorithms for nonlinear optimization is explored within the context of two contrasting algorithmic paradigms: “essentialist” vis-à-vis “populalion-based”. New CG variants are proposed that can close the current gap between theory and practice in the nonlinear conjugate gradients area. Our discussion also serves as an illustrative case study, within the setting of a particular class of optimizalion algorithms, of broader and more fundamental “algorithmic science” themes that are considered, in detail, in a companion monograph (Nazareth [18]). 
Keywords
Conjugate gradients, Multialgorithms, Algorithmic science, Population-thinking, Nonlinear optimization
