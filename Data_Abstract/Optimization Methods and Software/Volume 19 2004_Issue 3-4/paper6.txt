Comparison of optimal controls for a model in cancer chemotherapy with L 1- and L 2-type objectives
 Urszula   Ledzewicz ; Tim   Brown ; Heinz   Schättler ‡  ;
Abstract
 We consider a two-compartment model for cancer chemotherapy as optimal control problems with drug dosages as controls and with objectives which are linear, respectively quadratic. While optimal controls are bang-bang in case of an L 1-objective, they are continuous in the L 2-case; but they still ‘switch’ between values in the interior and values in the boundary of the control set. On the basis of sufficient conditions for strong local optimality developed earlier in this article for various cases of L 1- and L 2-objective, the advantages and disadvantages of using these type of objectives in cancer chemotherapy are discussed.  
Keywords
Optimal control, Bilinear systems, Cancer chemotherapy, L1- and L2-type objectives
