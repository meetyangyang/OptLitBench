Gradient methods and conic least-squares problems
Yu Xia;
Abstract
This paper presents two reformulations of the dual of the constrained least-squares problem over convex cones. In addition, it extends Nesterov's excessive gap method 1 [Excessive gap technique in nonsmooth convex minimization, SIAM J. Optim. 16(1) (2005), pp. 235–249 (electronic)] to more general problems. The conic least-squares problem is then solved by applying the resulting modified method, or Nesterov's smooth method [Smooth minimization of non-smooth functions, Math. Program. 103(1, Ser. A) (2005), pp. 127–152], or Nesterov's excessive gap method 2 [Excessive gap technique in nonsmooth convex minimization, SIAM J. Optim. 16(1) (2005), pp. 235–249 (electronic)], to the dual reformulations. Numerical experiments show that this approach obtains relatively accurate solutions for large-scale problems using less CPU time than interior-point method-based state-of-art software do and is more accurate than these software on problem instances possibly with dual degeneracy [F. Alizadeh, J.-P.A. Haeberly, and M.L. Overton, Complementarity and nondegeneracy in semidefinite programming, Math. Program. 77(2, Ser. B) (1997), pp. 111–128]. The approach extends easily to related conic convex quadratic programs.
