The Simplest Semidefinite Programs are Trivial
Robert J. Vanderbei;Bing Yang;
Abstract
We consider optimization problems of the following type: $$\min\left\{\mbox{tr}(CX)\COLON A(X) = B, X \quad \mbox{positive semidefinite}\right\}.$$ Here, tr(·) denotes the trace operator, C and X are symmetric n × n matrices, B is a symmetric m × m matrix and A(·) denotes a linear operator. Such problems are called semidefinite programs and have recently become the object of considerable interest due to important connections with max-min eigenvalue problems and with new bounds for integer programming. In the context of symmetric matrices, the simplest linear operators have the following form: $$A(X) = MXM^T,$$ where M is an arbitrary m × n matrix. In this paper, we show that for such linear operators the optimization problem is trivial in the sense that an explicit solution can be given.
