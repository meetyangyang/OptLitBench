Optimal Inspections in a Stochastic Control Problem with Costly Observations
Robert F. Anderson;Avner Friedman;
Abstract
A Brownian motion z(t) is known to be developing in time with cost f(z(t)) per unit lime. One chooses a sequence of times σn in which to inspect the motion. If it is discovered that z(σn) is in a given set A then we continue with the motion, whereas if z(σn) is not in A then we shut off the motion with cost y(z(σn)). Each observation at t = σn costs β(z(σn)). In this paper we find a sequence of inspections for which the total cost is minimum. This is done, in Part I, by reducing the stochastic problem lo a free boundary problem in analysis (called a quasi variational inequality), which is then solved. In Part II we solve a similar problem whereby, instead of shutting off the motion when z(σn) is not in A, we innovate it by bringing it to the origin at some cost y(z(σn)). Finally, in Part III we consider the case where z(t) can only be observed (at the times σn) with an error.
Keywords
controlled Brownian motion free boundary problem optimal inspection
