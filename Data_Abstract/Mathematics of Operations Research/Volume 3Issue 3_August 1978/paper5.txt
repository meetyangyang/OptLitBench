The Hirsch Conjecture Fails for Triangulated 27-Spheres
David W. Walkup;
Abstract
A triangulation Z of a 27-sphere is generated in which every sequence of adjacent 27-simplices joining a specified pair of disjoint 27-simplices revisits at least one vertex previously left behind. This shows thai the Hirsch conjecture, which asserts the existence of a nonredundant feasible pivot sequence between any pair of bases of a linear program, or equivalently a nonrevisiting sequence of facets of a simplicial polytope, is false when generalized in the obvious way to triangulated spheres. The complex Z is constructed from a complex with 400 4-simplices which has been shown to be shellable, and hence a 4-ball, by a computer computation.
