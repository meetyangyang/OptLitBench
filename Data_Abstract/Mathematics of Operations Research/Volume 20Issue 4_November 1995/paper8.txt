Diffusion Approximation for a Controlled Stochastic Manufacturing System with Average Cost Minimization
Elena V. Krichagina;Sheldon X. C. Lou;Suresh P. Sethi;Michael I. Taksar;
Abstract
We consider the problem of production control in a single machine, single product, unreliable manufacturing system facing a constant demand d. The goal is to minimize the expected average (per unit time) inventory/backlog costs. Under heavy traffic condition, i.e., when the average production capacity is close to demand, the problem is approximated by a singular stochastic control problem. The approximate problem can be solved explicitly. The solution is then interpreted in terms of the actual manufacturing system and a control policy for this system is derived. We prove that the resulting policy is nearly optimal under the heavy traffic condition. This policy is characterized by a critical level z0. That is, produce at maximal rate r when inventory is less than z0, and at the demand rate d when the inventory is equal to z0. The quality of the approximate control is also discussed by comparing it to known results, when they are available.
Keywords
production control average cost heavy traffic diffusion approximation
