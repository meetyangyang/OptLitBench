Discounted-Value Representations of Temporal Preferences
Jörgen W. Weibull;
Abstract
This paper examines the proposition that temporal preferences may be represented in terms of discount functions. An analytical framework is developed within which preferences are defined over a set of programs or prospects over time. Necessary and sufficient conditions for generalized discounting, discounting as well as exponential discounting are established for a class of program sets over discrete and continuous time.
