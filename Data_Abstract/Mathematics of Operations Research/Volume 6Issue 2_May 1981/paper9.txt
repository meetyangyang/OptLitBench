The Compensation Method Applied to a One-Product Production/Inventory Problem
Stephen C. Graves;Julian Keilson;
Abstract
This paper considers a one-product, one-machine production/inventory problem. Demand requests for the product are governed by a Poisson process with demand size being an exponential random variable. The production facility may be in production or idle; while in production, the facility produces continuously at a constant rate. The objective is to minimize system costs consisting of setup costs, inventory holding costs, and backorder costs. Given a two-critical-number policy, the problem is analyzed as a constrained Markov process using the compensation method. A closed-form expression is found for system cost as a function of the policy parameters. This cost function may then be searched to find an optimal policy.The production/inventory problem is related to a server backlog process for an M/M/1 system in which the server departs when the system empties, and returns when the backlog exceeds a specified threshold. The application of the compensation method to this server backlog process is discussed.
