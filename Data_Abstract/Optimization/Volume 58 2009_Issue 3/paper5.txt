Duality in static hedging of barrier options
 J.H.   Maruhn ;
Abstract
 During the past 10 years static hedging strategies for barrier options have been the focus of various studies. But while the primal optimization problem is sufficiently well understood, it has been unclear so far what the dual of the static hedging problem looks like. This article closes this gap by exploiting a linear semi-infinite reformulation of the static super-replication problem in the Black–Scholes model. As it turns out the feasible set of the dual problem consists of all measures which are price-consistent with the calls in the hedge portfolio–a class which is much bigger than the equivalent martingale measures which form the feasible set of the dual of a dynamic super-replication problem. Moreover, we prove the existence of an optimal measure solving the dual problem which is the finite sum of Dirac measures and analyze the duality result from a financial point of view.
