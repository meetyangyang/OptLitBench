An experimental study of bicriteria models for robust timetabling
 Dimitrios   Gkortsilas ; Christos   Zaroliagis ;
Abstract
 We conduct an experimental study for the timetabling problem in a public railway network under disruptions. We investigate three bicriteria optimization problems introduced recently that model the robustness of a timetable towards delays. We experimentally evaluated these models against various waiting time rules at stations. Our results constitute the first proofs-of-concept for these new robust timetabling approaches. 
Keywords
bicriteria optimization, robustness, timetabling problem, Pareto-optimal solutions
