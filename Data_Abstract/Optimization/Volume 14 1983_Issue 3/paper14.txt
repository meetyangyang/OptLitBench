Abstract
 We consider the system M/G/1/n–1 with the following service-discipline: A requirement arriving the system in presence of a free service place is compared with respect to the remaining service-time of the requirement staying in service. The requirement having the minimal service-time will be operated and the requirement having tile maximal service-discipline get the first waiting place in the queue. It is shown that in presence of this service-discipline the stationary-state-probability only depends from the mean-value of the distribution function of the service-time.
