Zur existenz maximaler werte bei quasiordnungswertigen abbildungen
 Fred   Alois Behringer ;
Abstract
 If f is a real-valued function which is upper semicontinuous on a compact subset of a topological space, then it attains a maximal value. There are similar results for partial-order-valued functions. This paper assumes f to be quasi-order-valued. Semicontinuity will be defined via level sets of the form {x∈x | α ≤ f(x)} or {x ∈ X | f <α}. Two order-induced topologies will allow the semicontinuity of f to be formulated as continuity relative to these topologies. In addition to the question of the existence of maximal values of f, the following will be discussed: properties of the order-induced topologies; semicontinuity of the greatest lower bound of a family of semicontinuous functions; partial semicontinuity of functions of the form f: x×Y→z.
