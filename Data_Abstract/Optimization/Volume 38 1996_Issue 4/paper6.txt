Motorway junctions as steiner points in a density field and generalizations
   G.   Deweb ;   B.   Helbig ;
Abstract
 For three, four, and five given points in a plane we numerically calculate STEINER points. In this problem, the “length” of curves in he plain is determined by the curvilinear integral over a given density function. possibly still depending on a transport flow on the curve by weights. In practice, such problems arise when points are connected by traffic routes. under consideration of the local dependence of the building, maintenance and ecological penalty cost. Unlike in the usual location problem there are certain cases where, instead of a STElNER point, we must find a STEINER point. we must find a “STEINER triangle”.  
Keywords
