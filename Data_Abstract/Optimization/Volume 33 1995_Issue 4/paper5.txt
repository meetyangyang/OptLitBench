Basis characterisation for linear multicommodity network programs
 Malcolm.C.   Pullan ;
Abstract
 In this paper, we consider the graph-theoretic structure of basic feasible solutions for linear multicommodity network programs (MNP). Previous studies of MNP have only portrayed part of this graph-theoretic structure. However the full graph-theoretic structure of basic feasible solutions is well known for the case of single commodity network programs (NP) and forms the basis of efficient methods for their solution. While NP is the most well known example, many other linear programs have been posed as problems on networks or hypergraphs, and the resulting graphical structure of the basic feasible solutions again used for algorithmic development, as well as to give a greater understanding of the problem. The basis characterisation given here for MNP is seen to be a natural extension of the basis characterisation for NP, thus shedding light on a well established problem.  
Keywords
Multicommodity Network, Linear Programming, Graph Theory
