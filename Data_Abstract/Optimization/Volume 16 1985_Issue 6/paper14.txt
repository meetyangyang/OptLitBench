  

Abstract
 The paper describes a game construction formed by a finite number of matrix games in which the sets of the first player's strategies coincide. This construction corresponds to the simultaneous execution of all the games entering into it, the player 1 being  obliged to choose the same strategy while the player 2 may choose his strategies independently in each game. Examples of games are given leading to this construction. A method is proposed for finding the value of the constructed game and the optimal strategies of player 2.
