  

Abstract
 We prove several theorems characterizing the set of Bayes decisions and yielding necessary and sufficient conditions for the optimality of players' strategy in statistical games with finite space of statistician's decisions. The family of probability measures entering into the statistical decision problem is not supposed to be dominable (i.e. we do not suppose that all the measures are a absolutely continuous with respect to some measure).The proofs are based on some new auxiliary notions of measure theory introduced in the paper. 
Keywords
Game theory, statistic game, Bayes decisions, optimal decisions in statistic game, AMS 1980 Subject Classifications, Primary: 90 D 35, secondary: 62 C 10
