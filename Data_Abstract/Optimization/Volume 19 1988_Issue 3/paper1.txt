Local minimality versus spongious minimality
   J.P.   Penot ;
Abstract
 A notion of minimality which is weaker than local minimality is introduced, hinging on the concept of sponge introduced by J. Tbjeiman ([4], [5]). This notion is stronger than the concept of critical point and close to it, as shown by an examination of its relationships with necessary optimality conditions. It can be interpreted, as local minimality with respect to the sponsions topology, a topology naturally associated with the given topology of the space and connected with directional convergence.
