Discrete silent duels with complete counteraction
   K.   Orlowski ;   T.   Radzik ;
Abstract
 The paper solves the so called discrete silent duel with complete counteraction. Players I and II taking part in this game, have to take m and n actions, respectively, in the time interval [0. 1]m.n-some fixed natural numbers. The actions are of silent type. It is assummed that if the player succeeds at a moment t ∊ [0, 1] then his opponent takes automatically at the same time t all his actions left. An accuracy function P(t) is common for both players. It is nondecreasing and continuous function with P(0) =0P(1) = 1. The optimal strategies for the players and the value of the game are found.
