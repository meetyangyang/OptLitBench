 In the paper a special non-homogenous Marxov process with continuous time and discrete phase space {0, 1, …} is investigated the intensities of transition of which are aij (t) = ai-j (t) for j > 0 and . The fundamental characteristics of this process will be found: pij (t 0, t) — stochastic transition function; pij (t 0, t) — stochastic transition function with forbidden transition into zero; distributions of value T  t,i  — moment of first reaching zero. A moving process with independent increments is introduced to investigate the stochastic transitions of the first process. A simple formula connecting  and Pij  is given. As an application of the received results an example of queueing theory is treated: the distributions of the length of the queue and of the busy period of a non-homogeneous Marxov-queueing-system with group arrivals of fixed size and group serving are found.
