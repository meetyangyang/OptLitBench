Abstract
 Me examine the structure of minimax strategic of player 1 in a two-person zero-sum game (ZPNS) with “analytical” kernel, if the set of pure strategies of player 1 is a compact sub-set of an Euclidean space.First we generalize a result mentioned by Karlin concerning minimax strategies in a ZPNS on the unit square with analytical kernel. Next we examine ZPNS playing un important róle in statistical decision theory and derive a result on least favourable distributions. The proof of these results is based on a theorem concerning the zero-set of an analytical function of several variables, which is proved in the first part of this paper.
