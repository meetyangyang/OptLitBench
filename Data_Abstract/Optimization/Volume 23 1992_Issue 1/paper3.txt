A Note on the core topology used in optimization
   J.   Klose ;
Abstract
 This paper investigates some interesting properties and applications of the so-called core topology in ℝ-vector spaces. The open sets of this topology, often called “algebraically open”, play a rather important role in optimization. Using the concept of the “algebraic core” of a subset a pretopology is constructed which is finer than the core topology, namely the “directional pretopology”. It is shown that all affine functions and all convex functionals are continuous with respect to the directional pretopology. Some examples for the utility of the results about the directional pretopology, the core topology and the concept of linear accessibility in “algebraic” optimization theory are shown. For example, the notion of directionally local minimality gives optimality conditions for directionally differentiable functions  
Keywords
Core Topology, Directional Pretopology, Linear Accessibility, Vector Optimization
