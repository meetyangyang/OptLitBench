Abstract
 A stochastic optimization problem is considered: n items are put on life test with random observation limits. The test is stopped for the item if a failure occurs or the observation limit is reached. The probability of observing a failure has to be maximized by choosing a suitable distribution of observation limits while the total time of test is given. It will be shown that for life distributions with increasing failure rate the observation limits should be random variables. For life distributions with decreasing failure rate equal observation limits for all items will be optimal.
