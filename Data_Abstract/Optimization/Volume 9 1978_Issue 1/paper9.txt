  

Abstract
 The conditions of realizability of two game-theoretic optimality principles are considered. The first generalizes the notion of core in cooperative games (the domination is considered only for coalitions from a certain set  of player's sets). The second, -stability, is defined for noncooperative games. A strategy vector is called -stable, if it is stable for all coalitions of . A correspondence between these principles is investigated.
