Decomposition of the polyhedron from Albrecht Dürer's ‘Melencolia I’ to a minimal pair of compact convex sets
 J.   Grzybowski ; D.   Pallaschke ; R.   Urbański†  ;
Abstract
 Two pairs (A,B),(C,D) of compact convex sets are equivalent if A + D = B + C, where ‘+’ is the Minkowski sum. In 15 Pallaschke, D, Scholtes, S and Urbański, R. 1991. On minimal pairs of convex compact sets. Bull. Polish Acad. Sci. Math, 39: 1–5.  [Google Scholar], the question was posed whether two equivalent minimal pairs are translates of each other. In 6 Grzybowski, J. 1994. Minimal pairs of compact convex sets. Archiv. Math, 63: 173–181. [Crossref], [Web of Science ®] , [Google Scholar], the first example of a three-dimensional minimal pair, which has an equivalent minimal pair not being the translate of the former, was given. Incidentally, the Minkowski sum of that pair resembles the famous polyhedron from Dürer's ‘Melancholy’. In this article, we give the decomposition of the polyhedron to a minimal pair of compact convex sets and prove that this pair belongs to a quotient class without the property of translation.
