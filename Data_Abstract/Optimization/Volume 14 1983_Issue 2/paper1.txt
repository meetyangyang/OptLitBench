Discrete and nondlscrete quasi eon vexlike functions and single-peakedness (unimodality)
 Fred ALois   Behringer ;
Abstract
 Donath and Elster's result on the equivalence of unimodal and x-convex (in the sense of Ponstein) functions in case of lower semicontinuity, together with some further results (relaxation of the semicontinuity condition etc.), is given for functions whose values are taken from a connected quasi order. The for-all-x condition can be replaced by a there-exists-anx condition. If the functions are defined on a discrete set, the equivalence only applies to a kind of unimodality where two (but not more than two) adjacent points can have the same functional value. This is the kind of unimodality that figures in Social Choise Theory in connection with the representation of single-peaked preference relations. Some 18 kinds of generalized convexity and their interrelations are discussed.
