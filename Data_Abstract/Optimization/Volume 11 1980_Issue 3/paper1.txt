Three improved yersions of scarf's method using conventional subsimplices and allowing eestart and continuation procedures
 Hoang   Tuy ;
Abstract
 The paper presents in a self-contained form three improved versions of Scarf's combinatorial method for finding fixed points. The first version allows to avoid the use of slack elements in primitive sets and hence to work only with conventional subsimplices. The second makes it possible to restart as in Merrill's algorithm, while the third is a continuation procedure similar to the homotopy method by Eaves.  It is shown that these Algorithms can compute a fixed point of every mapping satisfying the Leray-schaudbr condition and also of every mapping satisfying the inwardness condition or the nowhere outwardness condition of Halpern.
