Inequalities for the solutionsof nonlinear programming problemsdepending on a random parameter
 Ebu   Tamm ;
Abstract
 This paper deals with nonlinear programming problems which depend on values of a random vector ξ. Problems without constraints and with equality constraints are handled.  The treatment based on the formulation of the corresponding deterministic problems, in which the objective function and the constraint functions are the mathematical expectations of the corresponding functions.  It is supposed that solutions of the deterministic problems and the second order moments of certain functions of ξ exist. Differentiability of the functions is proposed.  Lower bounds for the probability that the deterministic minimization problem has a solution x ∗(ξ) and that ‖x ∗(ξ) –x ∗‖ < ∊ are given. For the case of unrestricted problems it is shown that these boundaries are sharp.
