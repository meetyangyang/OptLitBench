An analytical interpretation of the discrete approximation for the GI/GI/1 queue 1  
   T.   Kimura ;
Abstract
 This paper deals with the discrete approximation (abbreviated by DA) for the GI/GI/1 queueing system which was first studied by Kleinrock (1976). The DA is a kind of numerical method which approximates continuous random variables such as interarrival and service times of customers in the system by discrete ones. However, as pointed out by Kleinrock himself, there still remains in the DA an unsolved problem of how to discretize these random variables. It is shown in this paper that the DA is analytically interpreted in one sense as a piecewise constant or lattice approximation of Lindley's integral equation. Several important properties on the monotonicity, convergence and stability of the approximate waiting time distribution can be derived from this interpretation.
