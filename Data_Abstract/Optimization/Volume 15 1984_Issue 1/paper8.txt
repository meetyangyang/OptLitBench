Contributions to the hungarian method
 Ralph   Lehmann ;
Abstract
 It will be shown that the ”general concept“ of the Hungarian method (in some books this concept alone is called Hungarian method) does not supply an algorithm which in general terminates after a finite number of steps.  Moreover there is no hope for applying the idea of this method to higher dimensional assignment problems because the possible generalizations of the basic theorem of König Egerväry to higher dimensions do not hold.
