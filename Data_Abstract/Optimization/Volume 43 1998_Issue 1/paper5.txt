Transient solution of a queueing system with heterogeneous service rates and finite additional spaces using matrix technique
   V.   Sridharan ;   P.R.   Jayashree ;
Abstract
 A Markovian queue with heterogeneous service rates and two types of finite spaces namely ordinary and additional space, depending upon queue length is discussed. Whenever the system size reaches a certain length, the system adds an additional finite space probabilistically. The object of this paper is to find the transient probabilities of the number of customers in the system and the expected queue length of the system. Moreover, the characteristics like steady-state probabilities of the number of customers in the system when the additional space is not available are also obtained. Associating the cost with the rent of the additional space and the profit with each customer being served, the optimum size of the ordinary space as well as the optimum number of unit size additional space to be hired and the like are obtained. The above problem is discussed with a numerical example and tabulated  
Keywords
Queueing System, Additional Space, Heterogeneous Service, Ordinary Space, Optimum Size, Transient Behaviour
