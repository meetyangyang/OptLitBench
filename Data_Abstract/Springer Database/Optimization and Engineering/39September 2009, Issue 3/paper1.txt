Optimization of express line performance: numerical examination and management considerations
Tamás Koltai;Noémi Kalló;László Lakatos;
Abstract
Improving the waiting process at checkouts in stores is an important goal of operations management in the era of time-based competition. The paper presents a method for evaluating the effect of express lines on the waiting process. An optimization model is developed which minimizes the average waiting time in line with respect to the maximum number of items allowed in the express lines. The research is based on a real case of a do-it-yourself superstore, but the methodology applied at the store can be used generally.
Keywords
Queuing systems  Express lines  Sensitivity analysis
