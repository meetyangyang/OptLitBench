An efficient optimization procedure for designing a capacitated distribution network with price-sensitive demand
Amir Ahmadi-Javid;Razieh Ghandali;
Abstract
This paper considers a location-allocation problem in a supply-chain distribution network with capacitated distribution centers and customers with price-sensitive demands. The problem determines location, allocation and price decisions in order to maximize the total profit under two supply policies. Serving all of the customers is compulsory under the first policy, but is optional under the second. The problem is formulated as a mixed-integer linear program and solved by a Lagrangian relaxation algorithm under each policy. The numerical study indicates that the proposed algorithms are highly efficient and effective for solving large-sized instances of the problem.
