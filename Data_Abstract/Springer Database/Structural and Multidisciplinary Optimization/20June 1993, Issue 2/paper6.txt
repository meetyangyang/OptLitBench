Adaptive meshing — saving computational costs during the optimization of composite structures
M. Weck;T. Nottebaum;
Abstract
Structural optimization methods are widely used for determining optimum designs of composite structures. The high complexity of the problem and the large number of design variables, such as lamina thicknesses and ply orientation angles of a multilayer composite, makes it difficult to examine large structures with adequate computational effort. Using the finite element method for structural and sensitivity analysis, a large number of finite elements is necessary to achieve a certain accuracy of the analysis results, which also increases computational costs.
Keywords
