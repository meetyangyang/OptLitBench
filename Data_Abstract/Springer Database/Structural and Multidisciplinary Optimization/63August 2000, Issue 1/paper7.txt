On an effective topology procedure
R. Kutyłowski;
Abstract
The main goal of this paper is to find a way to minimize the number of steps in the topology optimization procedure. The material description of the topology optimization is employed. The technique presented is effective for the 0/1 mass distribution in the entire structure. According to the proposed procedure, a systematic adjustment at every optimization step is made whereby the optimum topology can be obtained in very few steps. The procedure involving the above adjustment cuts off the material property values that are below a certain fixed threshold value. The material property to which this procedure applies is the density of the material. Ultimately, in the n-th optimization step only a few design points have the material property in the interval between zero and one. These material property values are very close to 1. In the next (n+1) step only the 0/1 distribution is performed, but the total mass is usually a little less than the one assumed initially. The optimum topology of the structure is between the above two steps. The numerical implementation of the problem is presented using FEM for 2D examples, where the considered continuum domain and the FE mesh for this domain are fixed during the optimization, which means that the same algorithm and the same mesh are valid from the beginning to the end.
Keywords
Key words: topology optimization, compliance design, finite elements, Michell cantilever, 0/1 distribution
