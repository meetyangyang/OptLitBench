Optimization of structures under technological casting constraints
X. L. Deng;L. Proslier;
Abstract
Optimizing the shape of a cast part requires consideration of three types of criteria: geometrical criteria, which ensure the topological coherence of the part as well as respect the dimensions of the functional part; mechanical criteria, which guarantee the operational reliability of an object; and finally, thermal criteria, which ensure that a defect-free part is produced from the manufacturing process. The geometrical criteria, often expressed analytically, must be strictly adhered to. The mechanical criteria are evaluated using computation by the method of finite elements. Finally, the thermal criteria are obtained using solidification calculation results from the finite differences method. The purpose of this study is to demonstrate the feasibility and interest in an optimization procedure for cast parts, taking manufacturing criteria into account.
