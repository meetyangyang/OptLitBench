Cyclic Just-In-Time Sequences Are Optimal
Wieslaw Kubiak;
Abstract
Consider the Product Rate Variation problem. Given n products 1,...,i,...,n, and n positive integer demands d1,..., di,...,dn. Find a sequence α=α1,...,αT, T = ∑ i=1 n di, of the products, where product i occurs exactly di times that always keeps the actual production level, equal the number of product i occurrences in the prefix α1,..., αt, t=1,...,T, and the desired production level, equal rit, where ri=di/T, of each product i as close to each other as possible. The problem is one of the most fundamental problems in sequencing flexible just-in-time production systems. We show that if β is an optimal sequence for d1,...,di,...,dn, then concatenation βm of m copies of β is an optimal sequence for md1,..., mdi,...,mdn.
Keywords
