The Douglas–Rachford algorithm for the case of the sphere and the line
Joël Benoist;
Abstract
In this paper, we solve a conjecture proposed by Borwein and Sims (Fixed-point algorithms for inverse problems in science and engineering, Springer optimization and its applications, 2011) in a Hilbert space setting. For the simple non-convex example of the sphere and the line, the sequence of Douglas–Rachford iterates converges in norm to a point of the intersection except when the initial value belongs to the hyperplane of symmetry.
Keywords
Hilbert space  Douglas–Rachford algorithm  Global convergence  Lyapunov function
