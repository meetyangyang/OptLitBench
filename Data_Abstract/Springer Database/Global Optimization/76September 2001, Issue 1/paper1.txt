Piecewise-Convex Maximization Problems
Ider Tsevendorj;
Abstract
A function F:Rn→ R is called a piecewise convex function if it can be decomposed into \(F(x) = \min \left\{ {\user1{f}j(x){\mathbf{|}}j \in M} \right\}\), where fj:Rn→ R is convex for all j∈M={1,2...,m}. We consider \(\user1{f}j:R^n \to R\) subject to x∈D. It generalizes the well-known convex maximization problem. We briefly review global optimality conditions for convex maximization problems and carry one of them to the piecewise-convex case. Our conditions are all written in primal space so that we are able to proposea preliminary algorithm to check them.
Keywords
Global optimization  Sufficient and necessary optimality conditions  Nonconvex and nonsmooth problem  Piecewise convex function
