Nondifferentiability of an objective function at its point of minimum for most minimax problems
Alexander J. Zaslavski;
Abstract
In this paper we study a class of minimax problems \({\max\{f(x),g(x)\} \to \min, x \,{\in} \, R^n}\) where \({f,g \in C^1(R^n)}\). We show that the subclass of all problems for which there exists a point of minimum \({z \in R^1}\) such that \({f(z)=g(z)}\) and \({\nabla f(z)=\nabla g(z)}\) is small.
