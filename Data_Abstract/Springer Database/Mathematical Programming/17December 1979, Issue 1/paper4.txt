Combined phase I—phase II methods of feasible directions
E. Polak;R. Trahan;D. Q. Mayne;
Abstract
This paper presents several new algorithms, generalizing feasible directions algorithms, for the nonlinear programming problem, min{f0(z) ∣f j (z) ≤ 0,j = 1, 2, ⋯ ,m}. These new algorithms do not require an initial feasible point. They automatically combine the operations of initialization (phase I) and optimization (phase II).
Keywords
Phase I-Phase II Methods  Feasible Directions  Exact Penalty Functions  Constrained Optimization
