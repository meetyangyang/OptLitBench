The merchant subtour problem
Bram Verweij;Karen Aardal;
Abstract
 We consider the problem of a travelling merchant who makes money by buying commodities where they are cheap and selling them in other places where he can make a profit. The merchant ships commodities of his own choice in a van of fixed capacity. Given the prices of all the commodities in all of the places, and the cost of driving from one place to another, the problem the merchant faces each day is to select a subset of the cities that he can visit in a day, and to determine the order in which the cities are visited, such that the total profit is maximised. We call this problem the Merchant Subtour Problem. The MSP models the pricing problem of a rather complex pickup and delivery problem that was given to us by the Dutch logistics company Van Gend & Loos.
