Second-order conditions in C1,1 constrained vector optimization
Ivan Ginchev;Angelo Guerraggio;Matteo Rocca;
Abstract
We consider the constrained vector optimization problem min C  f(x), g(x) ∈ −K, where f:ℝ n →ℝ m  and g:ℝ n →ℝ p  are C1,1 functions, and C Open image in new window ℝ m  and K Open image in new window ℝ p  are closed convex cones with nonempty interiors. Two type of solutions are important for our considerations, namely w-minimizers (weakly efficient points) and i-minimizers (isolated minimizers). We formulate and prove in terms of the Dini directional derivative second-order necessary conditions for a point x0 to be a w-minimizer and second-order sufficient conditions for x0 to be an i-minimizer of order two. We discuss the reversal of the sufficient conditions under suitable constraint qualifications of Kuhn-Tucker type. The obtained results improve the ones in Liu, Neittaanmäki, Křížek [21].
