Computing the nucleolus of some combinatorially-structured games
Jeroen Kuipers;Tamás Solymosi;Harry Aarts;
Abstract
In this paper we introduce the ℬ-prenucleolus for a transferable utility game (N,v), where ℬ⊆2 N . The ℬ-prenucleolus is a straightforward generalization of the ordinary prenucleolus, where only the coalitions in ℬ determine the outcome. We impose a combinatorial structure on the collection ℬ which enables us to compute the ℬ-prenucleolus in ?(n3|ℬ|) time. The algorithm can be used for computing the nucleolus of several classes of games, among which is the class of minimum cost spanning tree games.
Keywords
Key words: TU-games – (pre)nucleolus – computational complexity
