Study on a supermemory gradient method for the minimization of functions
E. E. Cragg;A. V. Levy;
Abstract
A new accelerated gradient method for finding the minimum of a functionf(x) whose variables are unconstrained is investigated. The new algorithm can be stated as follows:$$\tilde x = x + \delta x,\delta x =  - \alpha g(x) + \sum\limits_{i = 1}^k {\beta _i \delta x_i }$$ wherex is ann-vector,g(x) is the gradient of the functionf(x), δx is the change in the position vector for the iteration under consideration, and δx i  is the change in the position vector for theith previous iteration. The quantities α and β i  are scalars chosen at each step so as to yield the greatest decrease in the function; the scalark denotes the number of past iterations remembered.
