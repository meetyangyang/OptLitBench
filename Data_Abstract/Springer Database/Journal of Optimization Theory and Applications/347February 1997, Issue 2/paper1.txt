Incorporating Managerial Thinking in Prediction and Control: Case Study of Market Penetration
S. Beifuss;W. Proskurowski;F. E. Udwadia;
Abstract
Managerial strategies, especially at the higher echelons of management, are often linguistically stated. This is because they need to be based on information which often defies quantification. Such verbal strategies and qualitative information have often been found to be difficult to incorporate in quantitative models. Thus, the quantitative effects of implementing one strategy as opposed to another have generally been difficult to forecast.
Keywords
Managerial strategies  qualitative information  quantitative predictions  fuzzy controller  market penetration  nonuniform influence parameter
