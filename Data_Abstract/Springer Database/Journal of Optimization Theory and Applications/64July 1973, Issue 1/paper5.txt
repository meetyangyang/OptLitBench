Control of the flux of substrate entering an enzymatic membrane by an inhibitor concentration at the boundary
J. P. Kernevez;
Abstract
The aim of this paper is to show that the simple gradient method is efficient when applied to the optimal control of a distributed parameter system. The system is a model of a biological membrane (with enzymes), and the problem is to approach a desired flux of substrate entering the membrane by acting on an inhibitor's concentration at the boundary of the membrane.
Keywords
Cost Function  Optimal Control Problem  Enzymatic Membrane  Distribute Parameter System  Adjoint State
