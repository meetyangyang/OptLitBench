On solving sparse band systems with three algorithms of the ABS family
J. Abaffy;L. C. W. Dixon;
Abstract
In this paper, we examine three algorithms in the ABS family and consider their storage requirements on sparse band systems. It is shown that, when using the implicit Cholesky algorithm on a band matrix with band width 2q+1, onlyq additional vectors are required. Indeed, for any matrix with upper band widthq, onlyq additional vectors are needed. More generally, ifa kj ≠0,j>k, then thejth row ofH i  is effectively nonzero ifj>i>k. The arithmetic operations involved in solving a band matrix by this method are dominated by (1/2)n2q. Special results are obtained forq-band tridiagonal matrices and cyclic band matrices.
