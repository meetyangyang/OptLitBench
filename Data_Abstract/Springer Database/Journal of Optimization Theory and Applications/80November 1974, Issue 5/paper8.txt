Value of information in two-team zero-sum problems
Y. C. Ho;F. K. Sun;
Abstract
The situation in which two groups of people have conflicts of interest is considered as a two-team zero-sum game problem. Two special cases of this problem are solved to illustrate that communication among members of a team may not be worth-while and extra information need not always be desired by decision makers. In the appendix, it is shown that the optimal saddle-point solution exists and is still affine for the general problem with quadratic Gaussian performance index.
Keywords
Team theory  zero-sum games  linear-quadratic problems  value of information
