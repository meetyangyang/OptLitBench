Optimization of a nonconservative elastic system with stability constraint
C. Sundararajan;
Abstract
The stability of an elastic system may, under certain circumstances, be affected by the distribution of the mass. By a suitable mass distribution, the critical parameter (e.g., load) can be maximized for a constant structural volume. Hence, the mass distribution can be used as a control function, the critical parameter being the merit functional. In this context, elastic systems may be classified into: (i) divergence critical systems, which always lose stability first by divergence for any control function, (ii) flutter critical systems, which always lose stability first by flutter, for any control function, and (iii) hybrid systems, which lose stability first by divergence for some control functions and by flutter for some other control functions.
Keywords
Aeroelasticity  elastic stability  Galerkin's procedure  structural optimization
