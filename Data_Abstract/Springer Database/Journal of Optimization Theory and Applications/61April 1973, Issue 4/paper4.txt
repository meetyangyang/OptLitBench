Duality relationships for a nonlinear version of the generalized Neyman-Pearson problem
H. D. Meeks;R. L. Francis;
Abstract
The nonlinear generalized Neyman-Pearson problem examined herein may be considered to be a calculus-of-variations analog of a type of bounded-variable convex-programming problem. By using an approach similar to the Lagrange duality theorem, but taking advantage of the properties of convex and concave functionals, duality relationships somewhat analogous to those of linear programming are developed. Necessary and sufficient conditions are obtained for a solution to the primal problem and for a solution to the dual problem. Also, an example is presented to illustrate some of the results obtained.
