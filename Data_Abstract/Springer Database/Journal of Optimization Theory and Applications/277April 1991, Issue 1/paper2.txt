Optimal vibration control of flexible spacecraft during a minimum-time maneuver
L. Meirovitch;Y. Sharony;
Abstract
This paper is concerned with the simultaneous maneuver and vibration control of a flexible spacecraft. The problem is solved by means of a perturbation approach whereby the slewing of the spacecraft regarded as rigid represents the zero-order problem and the control of vibration, as well as of perturbations from the rigid-body maneuver, represents the first-order problem. The zero-order control is to be carried out in minimum time, which implies bang-bang control. On the other hand, the first-order control is a time-dependent linear quadratic regulator including integral feedback and prescribed convergence rate.
Keywords
Maneuver and vibration control  flexible spacecraft  perturbation approach  minimum-time control  bang-bang control  time-dependent linear quadratic regulator  integral feedback  prescribed convergence rate
