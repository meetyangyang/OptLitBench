Functional inclusion and controllability of nonlinear neutral functional differential systems
E. N. Chukwu;
Abstract
Consider the nonlinear neutral functional differential inclusion$$(d/dt)D(t, x_t ) \in R(t, x_t )$$ (i), whereD is a continuous operator onIXC, linear inx t , indeed of the form (4) below, with kernelD(t, ·)={0}, and atomic at 0, andR is nonempty, closed, and convex. Here,I≡[t0,t I ] andC=C([-h,0],E n ). In (i), the derivative is specified in terms of the state at timet as well as the state and the derivative of the state for values oft precedingt. We use the Fan fixed-point theorem to prove the existence of a solution of (i) which satisfies two-point boundary values\(x_\omega   = \phi _0 ,x_{t_1 }  = \phi _1\), where φ0, φ1 belong toC. We next apply this existence result to study the exact function space controllability of the neutral functional differential system$$(d/dt)D(t, x_t )  = f(t, x_t , u), u(t)  \in  \Omega (t, x_t )$$ (ii). We present sufficient conditions onf and Ω which imply exact controllability between two fixed functions inC.
