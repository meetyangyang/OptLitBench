Disconnected optimal trajectories
Thomas L. Vincent;Joseph D. Mason;
Abstract
The Bolza problem of the calculus of variations in modern control notation is extended in scope to include situations in which a number of subarcs occur in a variety of ways. The subarcs are allowed to be overlapping and/or separated. This allows for several subarcs to occur in the same interval of the independent variable and also admits subarcs which are separated by jumps in the independent and state variables. In addition, the differential constraining equations and the integral quantity to be extremized are permitted to have different form from subarc to subarc.
