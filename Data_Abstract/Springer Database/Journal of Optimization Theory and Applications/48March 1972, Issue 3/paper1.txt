Optimal control of linear stochastic systems described by functional differential equations
A. J. Koivo;
Abstract
The optimal control is determined for a class of systems by assuming the configuration of the feedback loop. The feedback loop consists of an unbiased estimator and controller. The gain matrices of the estimator and the controller are so determined that the mean-squared estimation error and the average value of a quadratic cost functional, respectively, are minimized. This is accomplished by the application of the matrix maximum principle to a distributed parameter system. The results indicate that the optimal estimation and the optimal control can be computed independently (separation principle).
Keywords
