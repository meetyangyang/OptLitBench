The discrete maximum principle and the ɛ-technique
M. Y. Tarng;L. R. Nardizzi;
Abstract
A complete proof of the ε-maximum principle for discrete-time system is given. In proving the ε-maximum principle, the general linearization of the system equations about the optimum trajectory is avoided. Therefore, the requirements for the system equations are different from those of earlier works. It is shown that the ε-maximum principle under some mild conditions does approach the general discrete maximum principle and that the ε-maximum principle is always in a strong form. Thus, if ε is sufficiently small, the ε-problem can approximate the solution of the original problem and the difficulties inherent in abnormal problems can be avoided. It is also pointed out that the indeterminancy in the singular control problem can be avoided by using the ε-technique.
