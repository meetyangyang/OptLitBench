Offense Control Taking into Account Heterogeneity of Age
R.F. Hartl;P.M. Kort;G. Feichtinger;
Abstract
This paper studies the optimal tradeoff between the benefits and costs of preventing offenses and treating offenders. Based on a flexible age-structured epidemiological framework, a two-state compartment model is analyzed to reduce the prevalence of offending such as illicit drug consumption or violence. It turns out that, even in this highly simplified model, multiple stationary states exist. In particular, three different kinds of equilibria are identified, i.e., law and order, conservative, and liberal. The optimal mix of the control instruments is calculated providing interesting insight into the structure of the paths minimizing the discounted stream of social costs and expenditures for prevention and treatment.
Keywords
Optimal control  thresholds  Skiba points  distributed parameters  partial differential equations  multiple equilibria  economics of crime
