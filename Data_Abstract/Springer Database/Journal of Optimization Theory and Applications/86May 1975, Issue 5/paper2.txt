The effect of playability in differential games on the relation between best guaranteed costs and saddlepoint values
G. Leitmann;S. Rocklin;
Abstract
In a classical two-person zero-sum game, the decision pairs are members of a product space of admissible decision sets. Such a game possesses a number of desirable properties. In a differential game in which termination is required and a playability requirement is imposed on decision pairs, some properties of classical games need not hold. This is demonstrated by means of an example.
Keywords
Differential games  saddlepoint value  guaranteed cost
