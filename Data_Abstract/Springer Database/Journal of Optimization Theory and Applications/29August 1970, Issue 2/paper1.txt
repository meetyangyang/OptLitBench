Control-space properties of cooperative games
T. L. Vincent;G. Leitmann;
Abstract
If two or more players agree to cooperate while playing a game, they help one another to minimize their respective costs as long as it is not to their individual disadvantages. This leads at once to the concept of undominated solutions to a game. Anundominated orPareto-optimal solution has the property that, compared to any other solution, at least one playerdoes worse or alldo the same if they use a solution other than the Pareto-optimal one.
