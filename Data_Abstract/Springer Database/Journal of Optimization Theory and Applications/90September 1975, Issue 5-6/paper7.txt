Time-optimal control of multivariable systems near the origin
G. J. Olsder;
Abstract
This article deals with bang-bang solutions of linear time-optimal control problems. Linear multivariable systems are considered which have one or more control components. It is shown in which way the control components act together to make the system achieve the ultimate aim (namely, the origin in the state space) as quickly as possible. The theory only applies to initial positions sufficiently near the origin. Criteria are given which give the number of switches per control component.
Keywords
