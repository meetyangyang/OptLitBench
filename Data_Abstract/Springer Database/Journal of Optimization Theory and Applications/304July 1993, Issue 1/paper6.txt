Application of stochastic optimal control to the suboptimal design of thrust-mass profiles
Y. Yavin;
Abstract
Stochastic optimal control techniques are applied to compare the performance of identical medium-range air-to-air missiles which have different thrust-mass profiles. The measure of the performance is the probability of reaching a lock-on-point with a favorable range of guidance and flight parameters, during a fixed time interval [0,t f ], given that, during the flight, the trajectories of the missile are subjected to a variety of constraints including dynamic pressure constraints.
Keywords
Stochastic optimal control  thrust-mass profiles  dynamic pressure constraints
