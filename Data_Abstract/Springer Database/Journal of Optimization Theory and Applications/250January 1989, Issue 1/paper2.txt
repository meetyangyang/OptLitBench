A semistrong sufficiency condition for optimality in nonconvex programming and its connection to the perturbation problem
J. F. Bonnans;
Abstract
What happens when a nonconvex program, having a local solutionx0 at which the gradients of the binding constraints are linearly independent, but without strict complementarity hypothesis, is perturbed? Under a relatively weak second-order assumption (some nonnegative second-order terms are supposed to be strictly positive), the perturbed problem has, in the neighborhood ofx0, a finite number of local minima, situated on curves that are connected to some pseudo-solutions of the tangent quadratic problem.
Keywords
Nonlinear programming  nonconvex optimization  perturbation of nonlinear programs  sufficiency conditions for optimality
