Variational perturbations of the linear-quadratic problem
G. Pieri;
Abstract
A sequence of perturbations$$\begin{gathered}  \dot x = A_n x + B_n u,               \parallel u\parallel _{L^2 }  \leqslant 1,           (P_n ) \hfill \\      x\left( o \right) = x^o ,              n = 0, 1, 2, 3,..., \hfill \\ \end{gathered} $$ is given of the linear-quadratic optimal control problem consisting of minimizing$$\int_0^1 {((u - \tilde u)^T (u - \tilde u) + (x - \tilde x)^T (x - \tilde x))dt,} $$ subject to (P0). We assume that {An} bounded inL1 and {Bn} is bounded inL2. Then, a necessary and sufficient condition so that, for everyũ,\(\tilde u\),\(\tilde x\) ∈L2, and for everyx0, the optimal control for (Pn) converges strongly inL2 to the optimal control for (P0) and the optimal state converges uniformly is thatAn →A0 weakly inL1 andBn →B0 strongly inL2.
