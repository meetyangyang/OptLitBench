Exceptional Families of Elements, Feasibility and Complementarity
G. Isac;
Abstract
Feasibility is an important property for a complementarity problem. A complementarity problem is solvable if it is feasible and some supplementary assumptions are satisfied. In this paper, we introduce the notion of (α, β)-exceptional family of elements for a continuous function and we apply this notion to the study of feasibility of nonlinear complementarity problems.
Keywords
complementarity problems  feasibility  exceptional family of elements  zero-epi mappings
