Semipermeable directions and transition surfaces of a class of differential games
P. L. Yu;
Abstract
In linear and circular planar differential games, each semipermeable direction, except in degenerate cases, must be a tangent vector to one of the two extreme circular vectograms. Necessary and sufficient conditions for a tangent vector to represent a semipermeable direction are then derived. These conditions not only yield necessary conditions for a velocity vector to be optimal but also give a necessary condition for a transition surface to occur. Several sufficient conditions are then given for a transition surface to happen. It is shown that the adjoint variables are usually not continuous at double transition surfaces, so the switching function need not vanish on singular surfaces.
Keywords
Velocity Vector  Tangent Vector  Transition Surface  Differential Game  Degenerate Case
