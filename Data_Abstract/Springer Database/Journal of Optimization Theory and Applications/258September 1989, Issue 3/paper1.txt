Proportional navigation and the game of two cars
Y. Yavin;R. de. Villiers;
Abstract
A stochastic version of Isaacs's game of two cars is considered. The motion of the players is confined to the pursuer's effective operation zoneDP, and the cost function of the game is the probability of the event: {Before the evader enters his safe zone, the evader enters the pursuer's killing zoneKP, at somet, 0≤t≤T, or the evader stays at the domainDP−KP, for allt∈[0,t0],t0>T}. By numerically solving a nonlinear parabolic boundary-value problem on a generalized torus in ∝3, it is shown that, for a range of values of some parameter, a proportional navigation guidance law is an optimal feedback pursuit strategy.
Keywords
Stochastic differential games  game of two cars  proportional navigation
