Levels Sets Infimal Convolution and Level Addition
D. T. Luc;M. Volle;
Abstract
A sufficient criterion is established for the infimal convolution of two functions having connected level sets to share the same property without being exact. As a consequence, the infimal convolution of quasiconvex functions on a real line is quasiconvex. However, this is not true on a space of higher dimension, which is illustrated by an example in R2. Furthermore, connectedness of level sets and local-global minimum properties of functions are analyzed under level addition. Continuity properties of level set maps are also studied in relation with local-global minimum properties.
Keywords
Infimal convolution  level sum  connected level set  quasi-convex function
