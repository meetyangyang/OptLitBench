Nonconvergence results for the application of least-squares estimation to Ill-posed problems
T. I. Seidman;
Abstract
One standard approach to solvingf(x)=b is the minimization of ∥f(x)−b∥2 overx in\(\mathop \mathfrak{X}\limits^ \sim  \), where\(\mathop \mathfrak{X}\limits^ \sim  \) corresponds to a parametric representation providing sufficiently good approximation to the true solutionx*. Call the minimizerx=A(\(\mathop \mathfrak{X}\limits^ \sim  \)). Take\(\mathop \mathfrak{X}\limits^ \sim  \)=\(\mathfrak{X}\) N  for a sequence {\(\mathfrak{X}\) N } of subspaces becoming dense, and so determine an approximating sequences {x N ≔A (\(\mathfrak{X}\) N )}. It is shown, withf linear and one-to-one, that one need not havexN→x* iff−1 is not continuous.
