Solving in the affirmative a conjecture about a limit of gradients
C. Pontini;
Abstract
In this journal, Giannessi (Ref. 1) conjectured the existence of a convex function on ℝ, wheren ≥ 2, such that ∇f(te) exists fort>0,e = (1, 0, ..., 0), but limt↓0 ∇f(te) does not exist. Furthermore, he generalized the question by substituting the aforesaid ray with a curve having an endpoint at 0 (the origin) and by asking the question for the infinite-dimensional case. With the example in Section 1, we answer in the affirmative all parts of the conjecture. Section 2 (concerning curves) and Section 3 indicate conditions (asked in Ref. 1) under which the limit exists. In Section 4, an example peculiar to the infinite-dimensional case is given.
