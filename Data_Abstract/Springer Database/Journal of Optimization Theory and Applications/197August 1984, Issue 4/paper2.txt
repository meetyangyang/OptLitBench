On the gradient-projection method for solving the nonsymmetric linear complementarity problem
Y. C. Cheng;
Abstract
The Levitin-Poljak gradient-projection method is applied to solve the linear complementarity problem with a nonsymmetric matrixM, which is either a positive-semidefinite matrix or aP-matrix. Further-more, if the quadratic functionxT(Mx + q) is pseudoconvex on the feasible region {x ∈Rn |Mx + q ≥ 0,x≥0}, then the gradient-projection method generates a sequence converging to a solution, provided that the problem has a solution. For the case when the matrixM is aP-matrix and the solution is nondegenerate, the gradient-projection method is finite.
Keywords
Linear complementarity  nonlinear programming  gradient projection method
