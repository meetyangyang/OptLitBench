Second-order tests in optimization theories
C. Marchal;
Abstract
There are two main kinds of second-order tests in optimization theories. The simplest tests (Refs. 1–13), such as the generalized Legendre-Clebsch condition, require a local study around points of the trajectory of interest (generally, on a singular arc). Tests of the second kind (Refs. 14–22 and 51–53) are more difficult to use but also more efficient: they can be applied under various assumptions of convexity or linearity and generally require some integrations along the trajectory of interest (usually, these integrations can only be done by numerical methods). In favorable cases, the better tests of this second kind lead to either the conclusion of nonoptimality or the conclusion of local optimality.
