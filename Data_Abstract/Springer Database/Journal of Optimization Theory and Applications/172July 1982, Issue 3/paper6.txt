A stability theorem for symmetrically rational counterplanning
R. Kalaba;K. Spingarn;L. Tesfatsion;
Abstract
In the absence of contrary information, it would seem prudent for a competitor to attribute to his opponents the same level of rationality that he himself employs. In the context of a general, linear-quadratic, nonzero-sum, two-person game, it is shown that a counterplanning procedure consistent with this principle of symmetrical rationality always converges to the unique Nash equilibrium for the game.
Keywords
Counterplanning  symmetrical rationality  stability theorems  Nash equilibrium
