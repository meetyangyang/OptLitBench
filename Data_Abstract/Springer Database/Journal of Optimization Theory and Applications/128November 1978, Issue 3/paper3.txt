On certain functional derivatives
R. L. Hall;
Abstract
The functional derivative ∇Jy =Fy − (d/dx)Fy of the functionalJ[y] = ∫  a   b  F(x,y,y′)dx may be computed by the limit\(\dot x\)J y (x) = limΔσ → 0(ΔJ/Δσ), where Δσ is the area under a positive local variation atx, provided the height of the variation vanishes faster than the square of its width. This justifies the use of this limit by Gelfand and Fomin (Ref. 1).
