Optimum distributed feed reactors for exothermic reversible reactions
D. C. Dyson;F. J. M. Horn;
Abstract
Optimum chemical reactors for exothermic reactions are considered where the control is the distribution of cold feed along the reactor. In order to maximize the exit conversion for given reactor size and given exit flow rate, in general, the feed must be distributed along the first portion of the reactor in such a way that a singular trajectory is traced out. In the second portion of the reactor, the rate of feed addition must be zero. Certain transformation properties of the equations are utilized in order to facilitate computation. Results for an example are presented and are compared with the results for distributed indirect control.
