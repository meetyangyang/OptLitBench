Exact order of convergence of the secant method
M. Raydan;
Abstract
We study the exact order of convergence of the secant method when applied to the problem of finding a zero of a nonlinear function defined from ℝ into ℝ. Under the standard assumptions for which Newton's method has the exact Q-order of convergencep, wherep is some positive integer, we establish that the secant method has the Q-order and the exact R-order of convergence\(S(p) = (1/2)[1 + \sqrt {1 + 4(p - 1)]} \). We prove also that, forp=2 andp=3, the secant method has the exact Q-order of convergenceS(p). Moreover, we present a counterexample to show that, forp≥4, it may not have an exact Q-order of convergence.
