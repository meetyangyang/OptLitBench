On the maximization of a certain nondifferentiable function
V. F. Demyanov;
Abstract
Let us consider a function$$\begin{gathered}  \varphi (z) = \min \max f_{ij} (z), \hfill \\  \begin{array}{*{20}c}   ---  \\   {j \in 1,N}  \\ \end{array} \begin{array}{*{20}c}   ---  \\   {j \in 1,N_j }  \\ \end{array}  \hfill \\ \end{gathered} $$ where the functionsfij(z) are supposed to be continuously differentiable and real-valued on a set Ω ofEn,z∂En. The problem is to find maxzɛΩϕ(z). In this paper, it is proved that ϕ(z) is directionally differentiable. A necessary condition for a maximum is derived, and some numerical algorithms for maximizing ϕ are suggested. The results obtained can be applied for solving some problems in mathematical programming and control theory.
