Analysis of Random Restart and Iterated Improvement for Global Optimization with Application to the Traveling Salesman Problem
F. Mendivil;R. Shonkwiler;M. C. Spruill;
Abstract
The optimization method employing iterated improvement with random restart (I2R2) is studied. Associated with each instance of an I2R2 search is a fundamental polynomial,
\(f(x) - o_{0}x + p_{1}x^{2} + \cdots + p_{d}x^{d+1} - 1,\) in which the coefficient p k  is the probability of starting a search k improvement steps from a local minimum. The positive root η of f can be used to calculate the convergence and speedup properties of that instance.
Keywords
Random restart  iterated improvement  global optimization  traveling salesman problem
