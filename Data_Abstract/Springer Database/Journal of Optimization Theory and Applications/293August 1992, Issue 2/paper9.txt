Constructive steering control functions for linear systems and abstract rank conditions
R. Triggiani;
Abstract
For a linear system\(\dot x = Ax + Bu\) with bounded operatorsA andB, exact reachability tox T  or null controllability fromx0 can be characterized by an abstract rank condition, which extends the familiar finite dimensional Kalman criterion. We provide a novel elementary proof of the more demanding sufficient part, which is based on a new device and which casts new insight even in the finite-dimensional case. Such proof provides, explicitly and constructively, an infinite class of simple, steering control functions, of preassigned smoothness, including polynomial controls. WhenA is unbounded, one cannot expect at the outset that a rank condition will play a major, in-depth description of the steering problem. Yet, our proof admits appropriate extensions to this case also. Explicit examples point out, however, the limitations of the rank conditions, as well as the new pathologies that occur.
Keywords
Exact controllability  linear differential equations  Banach spaces  abstract rank conditions
