On the convergence of the projected gradient method
J. P. Dussault;G. Fournier;
Abstract
We study the projected gradient algorithm for linearly constrained optimization. Wolfe (Ref. 1) has produced a counterexample to show that this algorithm can jam. However, his counterexample is only ℒ1(ℝ n ), and it is conjectured that the algorithm is convergent for ℒ2-functions. We show that this conjecture is partly right. We also show that one needs more assumptions to prove convergence, since we present a family of counterexamples. We finally give a demonstration that no jamming can occur for quadratic objective functions.
Keywords
Nonlinear optimization  convergence of ascent methods
