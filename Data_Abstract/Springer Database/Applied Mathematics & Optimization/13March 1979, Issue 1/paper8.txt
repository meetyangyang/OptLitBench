Local controllability of observables in finite and infinite dimensional nonlinear control systems
H. Hermes;
Abstract
LetA, ℬ be evolution operators (possibly nonlinear) which act within a Banach spaceB andu(·) a measurable, real valued, control function. We study control systems of the form ∂υ t /∂t=Aυ t  +u(t)ℬυ t , υ0=ϕ ∈B. An observation of this system is defined to be a continuous linear mapg:B→ℝ k . Our main result gives a computable sufficient condition to assure that fort > 0 and sufficiently small, the observation of the reference solution (which corresponds tou(t)≡0) at timet is interior to the set of observations of all solutions at timet. An example to illustrate the theory is the local controllability, via tension, of various observations of a vibrating string.
