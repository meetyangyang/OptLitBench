Etude d'une Equation Différentielle Stochastique Non Linéaire avec Temps Local, Modèle Limité pour un Système de Particules avec Interaction à la Frontière
Sophie Weinryb;
Abstract
We describe a system of particles that can be considered as a simplified model of chromatographic problems: we are interested in the limit behavior of diffusing particles in a tube which contains a sticky support and the only interaction between these particles occurs on this boundary. More precisely the sojourn time of each particle involves the average number of all the particles which are on the boundary. Dealing first with the onedimensional case when the boundary is the origin, we prove the existence of the law of a Markov system with a penetrable boundary where the sojourn time is governed by the previous description. We show a phenomenon of propagation of chaos for this system where the limit equation involves a “nonlinear” term in the sojourn time. We obtain a result of strong and weak uniqueness for this equation. We conclude by extending these results to the multidimensional case when the boundary is a hyperplane.
