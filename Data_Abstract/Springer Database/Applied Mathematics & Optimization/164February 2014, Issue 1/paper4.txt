Unified Approach to Stabilization of Waves on Compact Surfaces by Simultaneous Interior and Boundary Feedbacks of Unrestricted Growth
Marcelo M. Cavalcanti;Valéria N. Domingos Cavalcanti;Ryuichi Fukuoka;Daniel Toundykov;
Abstract
Let \(\mathcal{M}\subset\mathbb{R}^{3}\) be an oriented compact surface on which we consider the system: 
$$\left \{ \begin{array}{l@{\quad}l} u_{tt} - \Delta_{\mathcal{M}} u + a(x)g_{0}(u_{t})=0 & \text{in } \mathcal{M}\times\mathopen{]} 0,\infty[ ,\\ \partial_{\nu_{co}}u +u + b(x)g(u_t)=0 & \text{on } \partial \mathcal {M}\times\mathopen{]}0,+\infty[. \end{array} \right . $$ If \(\mathcal{M}\) along with the localizers a, b and the nonlinear feedbacks g,g 0 satisfy certain conditions then uniform (but not necessarily exponential) decay rates of the finite energy of solutions can be established.
Keywords
Wave  Manifold  Surface  Variable coefficients  Nonlinear  Stability  Interior damping  Boundary damping  Decay rates
